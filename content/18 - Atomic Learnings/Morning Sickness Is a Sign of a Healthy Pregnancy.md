# Morning Sickness Is a Sign of a Healthy Pregnancy

Nausea and vomiting in the first trimester are driven primarily by rising hCG — the hormone produced by the developing placenta. Higher hCG levels are associated with a well-established pregnancy with good placental function.

Research consistently shows that pregnancies with first-trimester nausea have lower miscarriage rates than those without. This does not mean the absence of nausea means something is wrong — some women simply have lower sensitivity to hCG. But for those experiencing severe nausea, there is a bitter consolation: it is a physiological sign that the pregnancy is progressing.

This doesn't make the nausea less miserable. It makes it slightly more bearable.
