Quality infant care (0–12 months) is the most expensive and most waitlisted category of childcare in the US. In major cities, waitlists at reputable daycare centers routinely run 12–18 months. A family that begins their childcare search at 6 months pregnant may find that their preferred options have no openings for their child's first year.

The constraint is structural: infant-to-caregiver ratios are low (4:1 is typical for infants, mandated by many states), so centers have far fewer infant slots than toddler or preschool slots.

The practical implication: childcare research and waitlist enrollment should begin in the second trimester — ideally as soon as the pregnancy is confirmed. This is not premature. It is the correct response to the actual waitlist reality in most metro areas.
