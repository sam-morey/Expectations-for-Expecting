Approximately 15–20% of confirmed pregnancies end in miscarriage. The vast majority — roughly 50–70% — are caused by chromosomal abnormalities that prevent normal development. They are not caused by exercise, stress, sex, lifting something heavy, or anything the mother did.

This is one of the most important pieces of information for anyone who has experienced or fears miscarriage. The instinct to search for what you did wrong is universal and almost always unproductive. Random chromosomal errors during cell division, which increase with maternal age, are the primary cause — not lifestyle, behavior, or choices.

Recurrent miscarriage (3+ consecutive) warrants evaluation for specific treatable causes. Single miscarriage is typically a random chromosomal event.
