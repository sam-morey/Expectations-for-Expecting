Baby blues — tearfulness, mood swings, and emotional lability in the first days after birth — affect up to 80% of new parents. They are caused by the most dramatic hormonal drop in human physiology (estrogen and progesterone falling from pregnancy peak to near zero within 72 hours of delivery). They resolve on their own within 2 weeks.

Postpartum depression is a separate clinical condition affecting 10–15% of new parents. It begins at any time in the first year (not just immediately postpartum), persists beyond 2 weeks, and does not resolve without treatment. Symptoms include persistent sadness, inability to bond with the baby, loss of interest in activities, and intrusive thoughts.

The conflation of baby blues with PPD leads to undertreated PPD — women are told their symptoms will pass when they will not without intervention. The two-week mark is the clinical signal: if symptoms continue past two weeks or are severe at any point, contact a provider.
