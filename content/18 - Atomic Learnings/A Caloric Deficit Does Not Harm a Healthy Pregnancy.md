Pregnant women do not need to "eat for two." Caloric needs increase modestly — near zero in the first trimester, about 340 extra calories per day in the second, and 450 extra in the third. Overcorrecting and gaining more than the recommended amount increases risks of gestational diabetes, large-for-gestational-age babies, and postpartum weight retention.

The emphasis on "eating more" during pregnancy is culturally exaggerated. The emphasis on eating *better* — more protein, more folate, more iron, more DHA — is nutritionally correct. Quality, not excessive quantity, is the priority.
