# Month 7 — Weeks 28–31

Month 7 begins the third trimester. Baby is gaining weight rapidly and the brain is in an explosive growth phase. Physical discomforts intensify as the uterus presses further into the abdominal cavity.

---

## Baby This Month

- Week 28: Eyes open; blinking reflex; can perceive light and dark; brain developing billions of neurons; weighs ~2.2 lbs
- Week 29: Muscle and lung development accelerating; kicks are strong and unmistakable; gaining ~0.5 lb/week now
- Week 30: Lanugo (fine hair) beginning to shed; brain surface developing folds (gyri) — increasing processing capacity
- Week 31: Rapidly forming connections between neurons; all five senses functional; baby mostly fully formed — time now mostly for growth and maturation

**Size at month end:** About the size of a coconut (~16 inches); weighs ~3.3 lbs

---

## Mom This Month

**Common symptoms:**
- **Shortness of breath** — Uterus pressing against diaphragm; improves after baby drops at ~36 weeks
- **Heartburn and indigestion** — Stomach pushed upward; worse after large meals
- **Insomnia** — Discomfort, frequent urination, and anxiety all contribute
- **Pelvic pressure and heaviness** — Uterus weight increasing; ligaments under stress
- **Swelling (edema)** — Feet, ankles, hands common; worse in heat; sudden facial swelling warrants provider contact
- **Braxton Hicks** — More frequent and sometimes stronger; still irregular, unlike real labor
- **Fatigue returns** — Third trimester exhaustion is real; body working harder
- **Colostrum leaking** — Normal; use breast pads if needed
- **Skin itching** — Common; if palms or soles itch intensely, mention to provider (rule out cholestasis)

---

## Medical This Month

- Prenatal appointments move to every 2 weeks beginning around week 28
- Provider checks: weight, blood pressure, fundal height (belly measurement), fetal position, heartbeat
- **Tdap vaccine** recommended between weeks 27–36 — protects newborn from whooping cough before vaccination age
- **Rh-negative mothers** receive RhoGAM injection at ~28 weeks if Rh-negative
- Growth ultrasound may be scheduled to estimate fetal weight

---

## Sleep Strategies This Month

Sleeping becomes increasingly difficult. Key adjustments:
- **Left side sleeping** is preferred (improves circulation to placenta; reduces pressure on inferior vena cava)
- Use a pregnancy pillow (between knees and under belly)
- Elevate upper body slightly for heartburn
- Use the bathroom before lying down to reduce nighttime waking

---

## What to Do This Month

- [ ] Pack hospital bag (aim to have it ready by week 35–36)
- [ ] Finalize birth plan
- [ ] Set up nursery furniture
- [ ] Begin or continue pelvic floor exercises
- [ ] Receive Tdap vaccine (if not yet done)
- [ ] Take infant CPR class
- [ ] Confirm pediatrician selected
