# Monthly Development Overview

Pregnancy is typically divided into three trimesters, but tracking development month-by-month offers a helpful, structured way to understand both the baby's rapid growth and the mother's changing body. Below is a high-level roadmap of the 40-week journey.

---

## The 9-Month Roadmap

| Month | Weeks | Trimester | Key Baby Development | Key Mom Symptoms | Detailed Note |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Month 1** | Weeks 1–4 | First | Conception, implantation, blastocyst forms | Missed period, fatigue, mild cramping | [[01 - Month 1]] |
| **Month 2** | Weeks 5–8 | First | Heart beats, facial features & limbs begin | Nausea, fatigue, frequent urination | [[02 - Month 2]] |
| **Month 3** | Weeks 9–13 | First | All essential organs formed, fingers & toes | Morning sickness peak, mood changes | [[03 - Month 3]] |
| **Month 4** | Weeks 14–17 | Second | Active movements, baby can swallow, sex visible | Energy return, "nesting" starts, bump visible | [[04 - Month 4]] |
| **Month 5** | Weeks 18–22 | Second | Hearing develops, hair (lanugo) grows, kicks | Quickening (fetal movement felt), backaches | [[05 - Month 5]] |
| **Month 6** | Weeks 23–27 | Second | Viability threshold reached, lungs practice | Rapid weight gain, stretch marks, Braxton Hicks | [[06 - Month 6]] |
| **Month 7** | Weeks 28–31 | Third | Eyes open/close, rapid brain development | Shortness of breath, hip pain, fatigue | [[07 - Month 7]] |
| **Month 8** | Weeks 32–35 | Third | Strong kicks, baby positions head-down | Heartburn, sleep disruption, swelling | [[08 - Month 8]] |
| **Month 9** | Weeks 36–40+ | Third | Lungs mature, baby drops into pelvis | Pelvic pressure, loss of mucus plug, labor | [[09 - Month 9]] |

---

## Trimester Milestones

### First Trimester (Weeks 1–13)
*The Foundation Stage.* Focuses on cell division, implantation, organogenesis, and establishing the placenta. The risk of miscarriage drops significantly after the end of this trimester.
- **Key Guide:** [[02 - First Trimester]]

### Second Trimester (Weeks 14–27)
*The Golden Trimester.* Nausea usually subsides, energy returns, and the pregnancy becomes visibly noticeable. The 20-week anatomy scan is a critical milestone here.
- **Key Guide:** [[03 - Second Trimester]]

### Third Trimester (Weeks 28–40+)
*The Finishing Touches & Preparation Stage.* Rapid fetal growth and lung maturation. Mom prepares physically and mentally for labor, delivery, and postpartum.
- **Key Guide:** [[04 - Third Trimester]]
