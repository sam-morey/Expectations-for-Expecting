---

Month 8 is the final full month before term. Baby's lungs and brain are the last systems to fully mature. Prenatal appointments increase in frequency, and labor preparation begins in earnest.

---

## Baby This Month

- Week 32: Fingernails and toenails complete; skull bones remain soft and unfused (essential for delivery); fat deposits filling out; brain very active — processing, dreaming
- Week 33: Immune system developing; receiving antibodies through placenta; lungs nearly mature
- Week 34: Central nervous system nearly mature; if born now, most babies do well with minimal intervention
- Week 35: Kidneys fully developed; liver processing waste independently; lungs at ~95% maturity; gaining ~0.5 lb/week

**Size at month end:** About the size of a honeydew melon (~18 inches); weighs ~5.3 lbs

---

## Mom This Month

**Common symptoms:**
- **Rib discomfort** — Baby's feet in the ribs; improves once baby drops
- **Difficulty breathing** — Diaphragm most compressed this month
- **Pelvic lightning pain** — Baby pressing on nerves; sharp shooting pains down the legs ("lightning crotch")
- **Increased back pain** — Uterus weight and shifting center of gravity
- **Waddling gait** — Relaxin loosening pelvic joints for birth
- **Extreme fatigue** — Nesting energy and exhaustion alternate
- **Frequent urination** — Baby pressing on bladder again
- **Heightened Braxton Hicks** — Some women begin having very regular but irregular contractions

---

## Medical This Month

- Appointments every 2 weeks
- Provider checks: weight, BP, fundal height, fetal position (head down vs. breech check)
- **Group B Strep (GBS) test** at 35–37 weeks — vaginal/rectal swab; if positive, IV antibiotics in labor
- May include cervical check (provider-dependent); not predictive of labor onset
- If breech at 35+ weeks, discuss external cephalic version (ECV) with provider

---

## Preterm Labor Awareness

Preterm labor is contractions causing cervical change before 37 weeks.

**Signs to report immediately:**
- Regular contractions before 37 weeks (more than 4–6 per hour)
- Lower back pain that's new and constant before 37 weeks
- Pressure in pelvis or thighs
- Fluid leaking (possible water breaking)
- Bleeding

---

## Nesting

The nesting instinct — a powerful drive to organize and prepare the home — often peaks in month 8–9. This is normal and functional; channel it into practical preparation.

**Productive nesting tasks:**
- Set up and stock the nursery
- Pre-make freezer meals for postpartum
- Pack the hospital bag
- Wash and organize baby clothing and gear
- Prepare a postpartum supplies kit

---

## What to Do This Month

- [ ] Hospital bag fully packed
- [ ] Nursery complete
- [ ] Car seat installed (have it checked at a certified inspection station)
- [ ] Freezer meals prepared
- [ ] Infant CPR course attended
- [ ] Complete birth plan and share with care team
- [ ] Discuss breastfeeding plan with OB and schedule lactation consultation if desired
- [ ] Complete childbirth education
