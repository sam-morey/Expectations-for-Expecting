# Month 5 — Weeks 18–22

Month 5 is marked by two milestone events: the anatomy scan and first confirmed fetal movement. The pregnancy becomes real in a new way this month — visible from the outside and felt from the inside.

---

## Baby This Month

- Week 18: Ears formed; baby can hear voices and external sounds; yawning and hiccupping
- Week 19: Sensory development accelerating (smell, taste, hearing, sight, touch); vernix (waxy coating) forming
- Week 20: Halfway through pregnancy; swallowing amniotic fluid; sleep/wake cycles beginning
- Week 21: Eyebrows and eyelids fully present; bone marrow now making red blood cells
- Week 22: Grip reflex developing; lungs forming air sacs; face fully formed; looks like a miniature newborn

**Size at month end:** About the size of a papaya (~11 inches head-to-heel); weighs ~1 lb

---

## Mom This Month

**Common symptoms:**
- **Fetal movement** — Quickening typically confirmed this month; movements become stronger and more distinct
- **Bump clearly visible** — Growth accelerates; center of gravity shifting
- **Back pain and sciatic nerve pain** — Uterus pressing on nerves; Piriformis muscle tightening
- **Leg cramps** — Common at night; calcium and magnesium intake worth reviewing
- **Shortness of breath** — Diaphragm beginning to compress
- **Heartburn** — Stomach displaced; progesterone relaxing lower esophageal sphincter
- **Swollen feet and ankles** — Normal; worsened by heat, standing, sodium intake
- **Stretch marks** — Abdomen, breasts, thighs

---

## The Anatomy Scan (20-Week Ultrasound)

This month's major event. The anatomy scan is the most comprehensive ultrasound of pregnancy.

**What it assesses:**
- All major organ systems (brain, heart, spine, kidneys, stomach)
- Limbs and digits
- Face (cleft lip/palate screening)
- Placenta location and appearance
- Amniotic fluid volume
- Cervical length
- Fetal sex (if desired)

**Duration:** 30–60 minutes; baby cooperation affects completion

**If incomplete:** A follow-up scan may be scheduled — a common occurrence; not a cause for alarm.

---

## Medical This Month

- Anatomy scan: weeks 18–22
- Regular prenatal appointment (every 4 weeks)
- Weight, blood pressure, fundal height, heartbeat check
- Glucose screening coming up next month (week 24–28)

---

## What to Do This Month

- [ ] Attend anatomy scan
- [ ] Decide on finding out sex (if desired) — anatomy scan is the most reliable time
- [ ] Begin feeling and tracking fetal movements
- [ ] Start childbirth education class (many begin enrollment around this point)
- [ ] Finalize childcare options; sign contracts if needed
- [ ] Begin or continue building baby registry
