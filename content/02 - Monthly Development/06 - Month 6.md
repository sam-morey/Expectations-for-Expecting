---

Month 6 closes the second trimester. Baby crosses the viability threshold and the pregnancy enters the phase of rapid weight gain and brain development. The glucose test occurs this month.

---

## Baby This Month

- Week 23: Heartbeat audible with a regular stethoscope; viability threshold approaching (~50% survival with intensive NICU care at 23 weeks)
- Week 24: Official viability threshold (lungs producing surfactant; NICU survival rates increase significantly); weighs ~1.3 lbs
- Week 25: Brain growing rapidly; wrinkles filling with fat; nostrils beginning to open
- Week 26: Eyes can open for the first time; responding to sounds and light; practicing breathing movements
- Week 27: REM sleep established; brain very active; immune system developing with antibodies from mom

**Size at month end:** About the size of a head of cauliflower (~14.5 inches); weighs ~2 lbs

**Key milestone:** Viability — after 24 weeks, survival with intensive medical care becomes possible, though outcomes are much better with each additional week.

---

## Mom This Month

**Common symptoms:**
- **Strong, frequent movement** — Baby is active; may feel kicks, rolls, and jabs; partner can likely feel movement now
- **Braxton Hicks contractions** — Irregular practice contractions; feel like brief tightening; normal from mid-pregnancy on
- **Upper back and rib pain** — Uterus pushing up; baby kicking ribs
- **Symphysis pubis dysfunction (SPD)** — Pelvic girdle pain from relaxin loosening joints
- **Skin itching** — Stretching skin; if severe (especially palms and soles), mention to provider
- **Growing breasts** — Colostrum (pre-milk) may start leaking
- **Hemorrhoids** — Increased blood volume and pelvic pressure; common and manageable
- **Carpal tunnel symptoms** — Fluid retention compressing wrist nerves

---

## Medical This Month

| Test | Week | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Glucose challenge test (GCT) | 24–28 | Drink 50g glucose solution; blood draw 1 hour later; no fasting required |
| If GCT positive: Glucose tolerance test (GTT) | 24–28 | 3-hour fasting test; confirms or rules out gestational diabetes |
| Regular prenatal appointment | Every 4 weeks | Weight, BP, fundal height, heartbeat |

**Gestational diabetes screening:** ~10–15% of pregnancies; manageable with diet, exercise, and sometimes medication. Does not reflect pre-pregnancy diet or lifestyle.
