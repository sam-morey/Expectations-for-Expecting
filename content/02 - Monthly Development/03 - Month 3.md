---

Month 3 closes the first trimester. Nausea and fatigue begin to ease for most women toward the end of this month. The embryo officially becomes a fetus, and miscarriage risk drops substantially.

---

## Baby This Month

- Week 9: Tail disappears; officially a fetus (no longer embryo); eyelids fused; toes forming
- Week 10: Heartbeat detectable by handheld Doppler; organs functional; baby actively moving (not yet felt)
- Week 11: Fingernails and toenails forming; tooth buds present; baby can hiccup
- Week 12: Kidneys producing urine; thyroid forming; digestive system practice-contracting
- Week 13: Placenta fully functional; intestines move inside the abdomen; fingerprints forming

**Size at month end:** About the size of a peach (~3 inches, 7.5cm); weighs ~1 oz  
**Key milestone:** Placenta takes over hormone production from corpus luteum → nausea often improves

---

## Mom This Month

**Symptoms often improving:**
- Nausea decreasing for most (weeks 12–14)
- Energy beginning to return
- Less frequent urination temporarily

**Continuing or new symptoms:**
- Visible bump beginning (especially second+ pregnancies)
- Uterus rising out of pelvis
- Nasal congestion ("pregnancy rhinitis") — increased blood volume and estrogen
- Skin changes beginning: melasma, linea nigra
- Headaches (common; increased blood volume, hormonal shifts, dehydration)
- Round ligament pain as uterus expands

---

## Medical This Month

| Test | Timing | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| NIPT (cell-free DNA) | Week 10+ | Most accurate chromosomal screening; optional |
| Nuchal translucency (NT) scan | Week 11–14 | Ultrasound measures fluid behind baby's neck; screens for Down syndrome |
| First trimester screening | Week 11–13 | NT scan + blood markers (PAPP-A, hCG); risk calculation |
| Chorionic villus sampling (CVS) | Week 10–13 | Diagnostic (not screening); if high-risk result from screening |

---

## End of First Trimester

After 12–13 weeks:
- Miscarriage risk is <1% for chromosomally normal pregnancies
- Most couples choose to announce at this point
- Organ formation is complete; growth and maturation dominate from here

---

## What to Do This Month

- [ ] Complete NIPT or first trimester screening (if desired)
- [ ] Review genetic test results with provider
- [ ] Begin thinking about childcare (waitlists start now)
- [ ] Consider announcing pregnancy
- [ ] Review workplace parental leave policy
- [ ] Continue prenatal vitamin daily
