# Month 2 — Weeks 5–8

Month 2 is when pregnancy becomes unmistakable in the body — nausea, fatigue, and breast changes typically peak. Internally, the embryo undergoes the most critical phase of organ formation.

---

## Baby This Month

- Week 5: Heart forming and beginning to beat (detectable on ultrasound ~week 6)
- Week 6: Heart has all four chambers; neural tube closing; arm and leg buds appear
- Week 7: Brain growing rapidly; mouth, ears, and nose forming; liver producing blood cells
- Week 8: All major organs present in rudimentary form; fingers forming; embryo ~1 inch long

**Size at month end:** About the size of a kidney bean (~0.6 inches, ~1.5cm)  
**Heart rate:** 150–170 bpm (normal range; faster than adults)

This is the most critical window for organ formation. Exposure to teratogens (alcohol, certain medications, infections) carries the highest risk of structural birth defects during this period.

---

## Mom This Month

**Common symptoms:**
- Morning sickness (can be all-day nausea; peaks weeks 8–10)
- Profound fatigue — progesterone is at its highest; placenta still being built
- Breast tenderness, fullness, and darkening areolas
- Frequent urination
- Food aversions (common: meat, eggs, coffee)
- Food cravings
- Heightened sense of smell
- Mood swings and emotional lability
- Bloating and constipation

**For some:** Hyperemesis gravidarum (severe nausea/vomiting) — contact provider if unable to keep fluids down, losing weight, or showing signs of dehydration.

---

## Medical This Month

- **First prenatal appointment** typically scheduled around week 8–10
- Blood work: blood type, Rh factor, CBC, rubella, STI screen, thyroid
- First ultrasound to confirm heartbeat, gestational sac, and estimated due date
- After a heartbeat is confirmed at 8 weeks, miscarriage risk drops to ~3–5%

---

## Managing Month 2

**Nausea strategies:**
- Eat small amounts frequently (empty stomach worsens nausea)
- Keep plain crackers at the bedside; eat before getting up
- Ginger (tea, chews, capsules) — evidence-supported
- Vitamin B6 50mg up to 3x/day — first-line recommendation
- Unisom (doxylamine) + B6 — effective combination; ask provider
- Cold foods often better tolerated than hot (less smell)
- Stay hydrated even if eating is difficult
