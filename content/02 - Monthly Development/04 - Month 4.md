---

Month 4 opens the second trimester. For most women, this is the most comfortable stretch of pregnancy — nausea is gone, energy is back, and the bump is just becoming visible. Baby is growing rapidly.

---

## Baby This Month

- Week 14: Squinting, grimacing, and sucking movements; sex organs forming; lanugo (fine hair) growing
- Week 15: Ears nearly in final position; can sense light; bones hardening from cartilage
- Week 16: Nervous system functional; head more upright; eyes making movements behind fused lids
- Week 17: Fat deposits beginning to form; fingerprints developing; placenta now roughly same size as baby

**Size at month end:** About the size of a pear (~5 inches, 13cm); weighs ~5 oz

---

## Mom This Month

**Common symptoms:**
- **Round ligament pain** — Sharp, shooting pains on the sides of the lower abdomen; most common around weeks 14–16; normal as uterus expands
- **Growing bump** — Uterus is now clearly above the pubic bone
- **Increased appetite** — Nausea resolved; energy returning; caloric needs up ~340 kcal/day
- **Back pain** — Lumbar spine taking more weight and pressure
- **Skin changes** — Linea nigra (dark vertical line on abdomen); melasma; stretch marks may begin
- **Congestion** — Estrogen-driven; can cause nosebleeds
- **Dizziness** — Especially when standing quickly; blood pooling in lower extremities
- **Improved mood** — Most women report emotional stabilization in month 4

**What's subsiding for most:** Nausea, extreme fatigue, food aversions

---

## Medical This Month

- Regular prenatal appointments (every 4 weeks)
- Provider checks: weight, blood pressure, fundal height, fetal heartbeat
- Quad screen (if NIPT not done or declined): week 15–20
- Discussion of anatomy scan scheduling

---

## Quickening May Begin

First fetal movements — "quickening" — are often felt for the first time between weeks 16–20. In first pregnancies, this is typically later in the range. Described as flutters, bubbles, or light taps. Partner cannot feel movement through the abdomen yet.

---

## What to Do This Month

- [ ] Schedule anatomy scan (for weeks 18–22)
- [ ] Shop for maternity clothing if needed
- [ ] Begin researching childbirth education classes
- [ ] If childcare needed, start exploring and joining waitlists
- [ ] Start looking at baby registries
- [ ] Begin prenatal yoga or adapted exercise if not already
