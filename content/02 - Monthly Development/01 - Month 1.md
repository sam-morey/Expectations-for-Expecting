# Month 1 — Weeks 1–4

The first month is largely invisible — most women don't know they're pregnant until week 4 or 5 when a missed period triggers a test. Conception occurs around week 2, and by week 4, implantation is complete.

---

## Baby This Month

- Week 2: Ovulation and fertilization
- Week 3: The fertilized egg (zygote) travels down the fallopian tube, dividing into a blastocyst
- Week 4: Implantation into the uterine lining; the blastocyst splits into the embryo and the placenta
- hCG (pregnancy hormone) begins rising — this is what home pregnancy tests detect
- By the end of month 1, the embryo is about the size of a poppy seed (~1mm)

**What's forming:** The neural plate (future brain and spinal cord), the gestational sac, and the beginnings of the placenta.

---

## Mom This Month

**Likely symptoms:**
- Missed period (the most common first sign)
- Light spotting (implantation bleeding — normal, not all women experience this)
- Breast tenderness and swelling beginning
- Mild fatigue
- Mild bloating
- Heightened sense of smell

**Most women don't feel obviously pregnant yet.** Many symptoms are indistinguishable from PMS. Some women have no symptoms at all.

---

## Medical This Month

- Home pregnancy test positive: schedule first prenatal appointment
- First appointment typically not until week 8–10 (unless symptoms, history, or fertility treatment)
- Start or continue prenatal vitamin with folic acid immediately

---

## Important This Month

**Folic acid is critical now:** Neural tube closure happens weeks 3–4 — often before a woman knows she's pregnant. 400–800mcg daily is the standard recommendation; 4mg for higher-risk pregnancies.

**Avoid starting this month:** Alcohol, high-caffeine intake, NSAIDs (ibuprofen/naproxen), retinoids, and any medications not cleared by a provider.
