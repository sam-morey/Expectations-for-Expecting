Pregnancy produces more physical changes than almost any other life event. Understanding what's happening and why reduces anxiety and helps set accurate expectations.

---

## Weight Gain Guidelines

Institute of Medicine (IOM) guidelines for total pregnancy weight gain:

| Pre-Pregnancy BMI | Recommended Gain | Rate in 2nd & 3rd Trimester |
|---|---|---|
| Underweight (BMI <18.5) | 28–40 lbs | ~1 lb/week |
| Normal weight (BMI 18.5–24.9) | 25–35 lbs | ~1 lb/week |
| Overweight (BMI 25–29.9) | 15–25 lbs | ~0.6 lb/week |
| Obese (BMI ≥30) | 11–20 lbs | ~0.5 lb/week |
| Twin pregnancy | 37–54 lbs (normal BMI) | Varies |

**First trimester:** Minimal weight gain expected (0–5 lbs total)  
**Weight gain is not equivalent to fat gain** — most of the weight is baby, placenta, amniotic fluid, increased blood volume, and uterine tissue.

### Where the Weight Goes (at Term)
| Component | Weight |
|---|---|
| Baby | 7–8 lbs |
| Placenta | 1.5 lbs |
| Amniotic fluid | 2 lbs |
| Uterus (enlarged) | 2 lbs |
| Breast tissue | 2 lbs |
| Blood volume increase | 3–4 lbs |
| Fluid retention | 4 lbs |
| Maternal fat stores | 7 lbs |
| **Total** | **~28–30 lbs** |

## Skin Changes

| Change | When | Cause | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|
| Linea nigea | Week 16+ | Increased melanin from hormones | Fades months postpartum |
| Melasma ("mask of pregnancy") | Second trimester+ | Estrogen-driven melanin production; worsened by sun | Fades postpartum; use SPF |
| Stretch marks | Second-third trimester | Skin stretching faster than collagen production | Fade to silver over time; cannot be prevented |
| "Pregnancy glow" | Any | Increased blood flow to skin + sebum production | Ongoing |
| Acne | First trimester especially | Progesterone-driven sebum | May worsen or improve; gentle, pregnancy-safe products |
| Spider veins | Second-third trimester | Increased blood volume; venous pressure | Many fade postpartum |
| Darkening areolas | First trimester | Melanin; also helps newborn navigate to breast | Partially persists postpartum |

## Cardiovascular Changes

- Blood volume increases 40–50% during pregnancy — the single largest physiological change
- Heart rate increases ~15–20 bpm above pre-pregnancy baseline
- Blood pressure often drops in second trimester (vasodilation), then returns to normal
- Palpitations (awareness of heartbeat) are common and usually benign
- Edema (swelling) is normal from increased venous pressure and sodium retention

---

## Musculoskeletal Changes

- **Relaxin** hormone loosens ligaments and joints throughout the body — prepares pelvis for birth but increases injury risk
- Center of gravity shifts forward — alters gait and posture; increases lumbar strain
- **Diastasis recti** — separation of the rectus abdominis muscles as the uterus expands; common; requires specific rehabilitation postpartum
- **Round ligaments** — Support uterus; stretch rapidly in second trimester → sharp pains are common and normal
- **Pelvic girdle pain (SPD)** — Pubic symphysis loosens; can cause significant pain with walking or stairs

---

## Breast Changes

- **First trimester:** Tenderness, fullness, darkening areolas; Montgomery glands (small bumps on areola) enlarge
- **Second trimester:** Further growth; colostrum (pre-milk) may begin leaking
- **Third trimester:** Full lactation preparation; colostrum present; significant size increase
- Breast size increase of 1–3 cup sizes is normal; supportive bra without underwire recommended

---

## Postpartum Body Reality

Most pregnancy body changes are temporary, but the timeline is not linear:
- Uterus returns to pre-pregnancy size in 6–8 weeks
- Scale weight largely resolves over 6–12 months (breastfeeding can accelerate)
- Diastasis recti, pelvic floor, and ligament laxity require deliberate rehabilitation
- Skin changes (stretch marks, melasma) fade significantly but may not fully disappear
