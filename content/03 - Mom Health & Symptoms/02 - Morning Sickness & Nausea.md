Morning sickness is a misnomer — pregnancy nausea can strike at any time of day. It affects 70–80% of pregnancies and is caused primarily by rising hCG and estrogen. It is uncomfortable but in most cases a sign of a healthy pregnancy.

---

## When It Happens

- **Onset:** Usually weeks 5–6; sometimes as early as week 4
- **Peak:** Weeks 8–10
- **Resolution:** Most women see significant improvement by weeks 12–14 as the placenta takes over hormone production
- **Exceptions:** ~10–15% of women experience nausea throughout the entire pregnancy

---

## Why It Happens

The primary driver is **human chorionic gonadotropin (hCG)** — the pregnancy hormone that rises rapidly in early pregnancy. hCG directly triggers nausea centers in the brain. Estrogen also plays a role.

Evolutionary theory: nausea may protect the embryo from food-borne illness during the critical organ formation window when exposure to pathogens is most dangerous.

**Predictors of more severe nausea:**
- Multiple pregnancy (twins/triplets → higher hCG)
- Family or personal history of severe nausea
- History of motion sickness or migraines
- Female fetus (associated with higher hCG, though not predictive)

---

## What Helps (Evidence-Based)

### Lifestyle Adjustments
- **Eat before getting up** — Keep crackers, dry toast, or plain rice cakes at the bedside; eat before sitting up
- **Small, frequent meals** — Empty stomach worsens nausea; never let yourself get too hungry
- **Cold foods over hot** — Cold food has less smell; less likely to trigger nausea
- **Avoid triggers** — Common triggers: cooking smells, strong perfumes, meat, eggs, coffee, heat, stuffy rooms
- **Stay hydrated** — Dehydration worsens nausea; sip frequently; popsicles and ice chips count
- **Fresh air** — Open windows; step outside; moving air helps

### Supplements (Evidence-Supported)
| Supplement | Dose | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Vitamin B6 (pyridoxine) | 25–50mg, 3x/day | Strong; first-line recommendation |
| Ginger | 250mg capsules 4x/day, or tea, chews | Moderate; safe; widely available |
| Vitamin B6 + Doxylamine (Unisom) | B6 25mg + doxylamine 25mg at bedtime | Strong; FDA-approved combo (Diclegis/Bonjesta) |
| Magnesium | 250–350mg/day | Helpful for some; also aids sleep and constipation |

### Medications (Ask Provider)
- **Ondansetron (Zofran)** — Commonly prescribed; effective; discuss risk/benefit with provider
- **Promethazine (Phenergan)** — Antihistamine; sedating
- **Metoclopramide (Reglan)** — Motility agent; useful for vomiting
- **IV fluids** — If unable to keep fluids down; prevents dehydration

## Hyperemesis Gravidarum (HG)

A severe form affecting ~1–2% of pregnancies. Unlike typical morning sickness, HG causes:
- Persistent vomiting multiple times daily
- Inability to keep fluids or food down
- Weight loss (>5% of pre-pregnancy weight)
- Dehydration (dark urine, no urination)
- Significant impact on daily functioning

**HG requires medical treatment** — it does not resolve with standard nausea strategies alone. IV fluids, prescription antiemetics, and occasionally hospitalization are needed.

**Contact your provider if:**
- You cannot keep any liquids down for 24+ hours
- You are losing weight
- You see signs of dehydration (dark urine, dizziness, no urination)
- Nausea is preventing basic functioning

---

## Emotional Impact

Severe nausea during early pregnancy is emotionally as well as physically exhausting. Many women feel isolated — the pregnancy is often not announced yet, so the suffering is private. HG in particular is associated with depression and anxiety.

What to say to yourself: The nausea is a sign of a healthy pregnancy. It will not last forever. Most women feel dramatically better by week 12–14.
