## First Trimester Symptoms (Weeks 1–13)

| Symptom | Cause | Management |
|---|---|---|
| Nausea / morning sickness | Rising hCG and estrogen | Small meals, ginger, B6, Unisom, time |
| Fatigue | Progesterone; placenta building | Rest; no fix; resolves second trimester |
| Breast tenderness | Estrogen and progesterone | Supportive bra; cold compress |
| Frequent urination | hCG; growing uterus; increased kidney filtration | Normal; reduce fluids after 6pm for sleep |
| Food aversions | Heightened olfactory sensitivity from hormones | Eat what you can tolerate; focus on prenatal vitamin |
| Bloating/constipation | Progesterone slows gut motility | Fiber, water, gentle movement |
| Headaches | Increased blood volume; hormonal changes; dehydration | Hydration; Tylenol (acetaminophen) only; ice pack |
| Light spotting (implantation) | Implantation ~day 6–10 | Normal if light; contact provider if heavy |
| Mood swings | Rapid hormonal changes | Expected; communication with partner |
| Metallic taste | hCG-related; unknown mechanism | Time; strong flavors can help |
| Excessive salivation | Ptyalism gravidarum; hormonal | Time; sour candies may reduce saliva |

## Second Trimester Symptoms (Weeks 14–27)

| Symptom | Cause | Management |
|---|---|---|
| Round ligament pain | Uterus stretching; ligaments pulling | Slow movements; warm compress; resolve on their own |
| Back pain | Shifted center of gravity; relaxin loosening joints | Posture; exercise; prenatal belt; PT |
| Skin changes (linea nigra, melasma) | Increased melanin from hormones | Sunscreen; fades postpartum |
| Stretch marks | Rapid skin stretching | Moisturize; genetic predisposition is main factor |
| Nasal congestion | Increased estrogen and blood volume | Saline rinse; humidifier; no decongestants without MD approval |
| Heartburn | Progesterone relaxing LES; stomach displacement | Small meals; upright after eating; antacids |
| Leg cramps | Decreased calcium/magnesium; circulation | Stretching; magnesium; hydration; calcium |
| Dizziness | Blood pooling; low blood pressure; anemia | Rise slowly; stay hydrated; iron intake |
| Braxton Hicks (early) | Uterus practice contractions | Normal; irregular; hydrate; rest |
| Swollen feet/ankles | Increased fluid volume and venous pressure | Elevate; compression socks; limit sodium; stay cool |

## Third Trimester Symptoms (Weeks 28–40)

| Symptom | Cause | Management |
|---|---|---|
| Shortness of breath | Diaphragm compressed by uterus | Slow down; sleep propped up; improves after baby drops |
| Severe heartburn | Stomach fully displaced | Small meals; sleep elevated; Zantac/Pepcid per provider |
| Pelvic pressure | Baby's weight and position | Prenatal belt; pelvic floor PT; rest |
| Insomnia | Discomfort; urination; anxiety | Pregnancy pillow; side sleeping; white noise; magnesium |
| Lightning crotch | Baby pressing on pelvic nerves | No treatment; normal; changes with baby's position |
| Carpal tunnel | Fluid retention compressing median nerve | Wrist brace at night; hand elevation; usually resolves postpartum |
| Hemorrhoids | Increased pelvic blood flow and pressure | Stool softener; sitz bath; Tucks pads; fiber |
| Varicose veins | Increased blood volume and venous pressure | Compression stockings; elevate legs |
| Pelvic girdle pain (SPD) | Relaxin loosening pubic symphysis | PT; support belt; avoid wide-stance movements |
| Itching (mild) | Skin stretching; dry skin | Moisturize; avoid hot showers |

## When to Call Your Provider

**Call same day:**
- Bleeding (more than light spotting)
- Severe headache that doesn't respond to Tylenol
- Vision changes (blurring, spots, flashing lights)
- Significant facial or hand swelling (sudden onset)
- Burning with urination (UTI common in pregnancy; treat promptly)
- Fever over 100.4°F

**Go to L&D immediately:**
- Signs of preeclampsia: severe headache + vision changes + upper right abdominal pain + sudden swelling
- Heavy vaginal bleeding
- Water breaking
- Reduced or absent fetal movement (after 24 weeks)
- Regular contractions before 37 weeks
