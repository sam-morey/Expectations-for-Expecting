Guidance on safe sleeping positions during pregnancy, with context on why the recommendations exist and what the risks actually are.

---

## The Recommendation

**Sleep on your side** — left side preferred, right side acceptable.

This recommendation becomes relevant around **20 weeks**, when the uterus is large enough to compress major blood vessels when lying flat.

---

## The Anatomy Behind the Recommendation

The **inferior vena cava (IVC)** is a large vein running on the right side of the spine, returning blood from the lower body to the heart. In the supine (flat on back) position after ~20 weeks, the growing uterus can press against the IVC, reducing venous return and potentially decreasing blood flow to the placenta.

**Why left side is preferred over right:**
- The IVC is on the right side; left-side sleeping moves the uterus away from it
- Some studies suggest slightly better placental blood flow with left-side sleeping
- Left side also places the uterus away from the descending aorta

**Practical reality:** The difference between left and right side sleeping is small in healthy pregnancies. Right side sleeping is not dangerous — it's simply second-best.

---

## What Happens If You Wake Up on Your Back?

**Don't panic.**

- Your body's discomfort response (lightheadedness, shortness of breath) will naturally prompt you to roll over
- Occasional supine positioning during sleep does not harm the baby
- Deliberately sleeping on your back for extended periods is the concern — not occasional position changes during sleep

**Studies on this:** Some research suggests an association between late pregnancy back sleeping and adverse outcomes, but causality is not established and the absolute risk to individual pregnancies is very low.

**If you find yourself on your back every night:** Use a wedge or pillow behind your back to prevent fully rolling supine. A pool noodle sewn into the back of a sleep shirt is a low-tech solution.

---

## Third Trimester Sleep Setup

**Optimal setup:**
- **Left side** as starting position
- **Pillow between knees** — keeps hips neutral; reduces low back and SI joint pain
- **Pillow supporting the belly** from below (wedge or regular pillow)
- **Upper body slightly elevated** if heartburn or shortness of breath is present (1–2 pillows under head and shoulders; or upper-body-length wedge)

**Alternative if hip pain is severe:** Some women find semi-reclined sleeping on a recliner or propped-up position more comfortable in the third trimester. This is safe.

## Infant Safe Sleep (For Reference)

While this note covers maternal sleep positioning, the infant safe sleep guidelines are distinct:

- **Baby sleeps on their BACK** (the opposite of maternal recommendation)
- Firm, flat surface
- No soft bedding, pillows, or bumpers in sleep space
- Same room as parents for first 6–12 months (per AAP)
