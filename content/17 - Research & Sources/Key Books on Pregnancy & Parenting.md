Curated reading list organized by category. Not an exhaustive list — a focused one of the books most worth reading.

---

## Pregnancy and Birth

| Book | Author | Why Read It |
|---|---|---|
| *What to Expect When You're Expecting* | Heidi Murkoff | The classic reference; comprehensive week-by-week; good for practical questions |
| *Expecting Better* | Emily Oster | Economist who analyzed the evidence behind pregnancy guidelines; essential for evidence-based decision-making |
| *The Mama Natural Week-by-Week Guide* | Genevieve Howland | Natural pregnancy perspective; detailed weekly guide |
| *Ina May's Guide to Childbirth* | Ina May Gaskin | Birth confidence; midwifery perspective; powerful birth stories |
| *HypnoBirthing* | Marie Mongan | Fear-release approach to birth preparation; widely used |

---

## Labor and Birth Preparation

| Book | Author | Why Read It |
|---|---|---|
| *The Birth Partner* | Penny Simkin | Written for support people; best practical labor support guide |
| *Natural Hospital Birth* | Cynthia Gabriel | Navigating hospital birth with natural preferences |
| *Birthing From Within* | Pam England | Art and emotional preparation for birth |

---

## Newborn and Postpartum

| Book | Author | Why Read It |
|---|---|---|
| *The Happiest Baby on the Block* | Harvey Karp | The 5 S's; most effective newborn soothing framework |
| *Bringing Up Bébé* | Pamela Druckerman | French approach to infant autonomy and sleep; different cultural lens |
| *The Fourth Trimester* | Kimberly Ann Johnson | Postpartum recovery; physical and emotional healing |
| *This Is Not What I Expected* | Karen Kleiman | Overcoming postpartum depression |

## Baby Sleep

| Book | Author | Why Read It |
|---|---|---|
| *Precious Little Sleep* | Alexis Dubner | Practical, evidence-based, non-judgmental approach to infant sleep |
| *The Sleep Easy Solution* | Jennifer Waldburger | Step-by-step sleep training guide |
| *Healthy Sleep Habits, Happy Child* | Marc Weissbluth | Evidence-based; detailed; reference for specific ages |

## Parenting Philosophy and Child Development

| Book | Author | Why Read It |
|---|---|---|
| *The Whole-Brain Child* | Daniel Siegel & Tina Payne Bryson | Brain science applied to parenting; practical; evidence-based |
| *How to Talk So Little Kids Will Listen* | Faber & King | Communication with toddlers; deeply practical |
| *Brain Rules for Baby* | John Medina | Neuroscience of infant and child development; debunks myths |
| *The Gardener and the Carpenter* | Alison Gopnik | Philosophy of child development; why kids learn differently than we think |
| *No-Drama Discipline* | Siegel & Bryson | Follow-up to Whole-Brain Child; discipline philosophy |

| Book | Author | Why Read It |
