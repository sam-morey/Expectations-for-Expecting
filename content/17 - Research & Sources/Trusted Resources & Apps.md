# Trusted Resources & Apps

Curated reference for evidence-based pregnancy and parenting information — and tools for tracking, community, and information.

---

## Authoritative Medical Sources

| Organization | Website | What It Covers |
|---|---|---|
| ACOG (American College of OB/GYN) | acog.org | Clinical guidelines; FAQs for patients; evidence-based recommendations |
| CDC | cdc.gov/pregnancy | Vaccination, safety, public health |
| AAP (American Academy of Pediatrics) | healthychildren.org | All things pediatric; safe sleep; feeding; development |
| NICHD (NIH) | nichd.nih.gov | Research on fetal and child development |
| March of Dimes | marchofdimes.org | Preterm birth; birth defects; prenatal health |

## Evidence-Based Pregnancy Information

| Resource | Why It's Worth It |
|---|---|
| Emily Oster's *ParentData* (parentdata.org) | Evidence-based analysis of pregnancy and parenting research; accessible writing; newsletter worth subscribing to |
| Evidenced Based Birth (evidencebasedbirth.com) | In-depth analysis of birth-related research; great for understanding options during labor |
| ACOG Patient FAQs | Reliable clinical guidance in plain language |

## Apps for Pregnancy Tracking

| App | Best For |
|---|---|
| **Ovia Pregnancy** | Week-by-week development; symptom tracking; appointment reminders |
| **The Bump** | Week-by-week; community; birth plan builder |
| **Glow Nurture** | Data tracking; week-by-week info |
| **BabyCenter** | Week-by-week; community forums; expert articles |
| **Papaya** | Clean interface; excellent week-by-week detail |

**Choose one and use it consistently.** They largely overlap; pick based on interface preference.
