# Birth Plan Guide

A birth plan communicates your preferences and values to your care team. It is not a contract or a guarantee — labor is unpredictable — but it ensures your voice is heard and reduces in-the-moment decision-making under stress.

---

## What a Birth Plan Is (and Isn't)

**Is:** A clear, concise expression of your preferences for labor, delivery, and immediate postpartum — shared with your care team in advance.

**Is not:** A script for what will happen. Plans change when clinical circumstances require it. The most useful birth plans are flexible and include preferences for multiple scenarios.

---

## When to Write It

Start drafting at weeks 28–32; finalize by week 36. Share with your OB/midwife before you're in labor so preferences are noted in your chart.

---

## What to Include

### Labor Preferences

- **Monitoring:** Do you prefer continuous electronic fetal monitoring or intermittent monitoring (if low-risk)?
- **IV access:** Routine IV vs. hep-lock (saline lock) only for low-risk labor?
- **Freedom of movement:** Do you want to be free to walk, use the tub, use a birth ball?
- **Induction interventions:** Preferences around AROM (artificial rupture of membranes), Pitocin augmentation?
- **Support people:** Who is present? Doula?

### Pain Management

- Your preferences for pain management (with flexibility)
- First-choice approach; backup preferences
- "Do not offer me pain medication unless I ask" — if unmedicated is the goal
- "I plan to request an epidural early" — if that's the choice

### Pushing and Birth

- **Directed pushing vs. laboring down:** Do you prefer coached pushing or following your own urges?
- **Positions:** Would you like to try different positions (side-lying, hands-and-knees, squatting)?
- **Mirror:** Would you like to see baby crowning?
- **Who announces sex:** If not yet known, who announces it?
- **Cord:** Who cuts the cord? Delayed cord clamping preference? (Standard recommendation: 30–60 seconds minimum delay)
- **Immediate skin-to-skin:** Yes; how long before assessment and measurements?

### Cesarean Birth (If Needed)

- Preferences if an unplanned cesarean becomes necessary
- Gentle/family-centered cesarean preferences (lowering drape, immediate skin-to-skin, music)
- Who is present in the OR?

### Immediate Postpartum

- **Skin-to-skin:** Uninterrupted time before baby is weighed and measured?
- **Golden hour:** An uninterrupted first hour with baby?
- **Breastfeeding initiation:** Immediate offer?
- **Delayed newborn procedures:** Can eye ointment, Vitamin K, and hearing screen wait until after the golden hour?
- **Placenta:** Any preferences (keep it, encapsulation)?

---

## Format Tips

- **Keep it to one page.** Your nurse will read it during a busy shift; brevity is respectful.
- Use bullet points, not paragraphs
- Prioritize — if something is truly important to you, make it clear; don't bury it in a list of 30 preferences
- Include a brief note of acknowledgment: "We understand that plans may change and we trust our care team to keep us informed and make decisions in our best interest."

---

## Sample Birth Plan Template

> **[Names]'s Birth Preferences**  
> Provider: _____ / Hospital: _____  
> 
> **Labor:** Freedom of movement; intermittent monitoring if low-risk; hep-lock only; please ask before interventions  
> 
> **Pain management:** We plan to try [unmedicated/early epidural/other]; please [offer/do not offer] medication unless requested  
> 
> **Birth:** Delayed cord clamping (min 60 seconds); [Partner/Doula] to cut cord; immediate skin-to-skin  
> 
> **Newborn:** Delay routine procedures until after golden hour; breastfeeding support as soon as possible  
> 
> We trust your expertise and ask to be kept informed of all decisions. Thank you.
