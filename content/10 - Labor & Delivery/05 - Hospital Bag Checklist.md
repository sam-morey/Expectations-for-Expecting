Pack by week 36. Keep the bag in the car or near the door for the last weeks of pregnancy.

## For the Birthing Person

### Comfort and Labor
- [ ] Birth plan (multiple copies)
- [ ] Earbuds / headphones (music, podcasts, meditation)
- [ ] Playlist prepared
- [ ] Phone charger + portable power bank
- [ ] Lip balm (frequent lip licking and gas dries lips)
- [ ] Hair ties; hair clips
- [ ] Massage oil or lotion (for counter-pressure)
- [ ] Tennis ball or massage tool (back pressure)
- [ ] Warm socks (hospital floors; feet often cold)
- [ ] Own pillow (hospitals pillows are flat; bring a brightly colored case so it doesn't get lost)
- [ ] Light robe or button-front pajamas (easier with skin-to-skin and breastfeeding)
- [ ] Comfortable slippers or flip-flops (shower)
- [ ] Eye mask and earplugs (postpartum recovery)
- [ ] Snacks for labor (honey sticks, applesauce pouches, granola bars, gummies — light, easy to eat)
- [ ] Water bottle or large tumbler with straw (drinking while lying down)
- [ ] Fan or misting spray (labor overheating)

### Postpartum
- [ ] Loose, comfortable outfit to wear home (maternity size — body same size as ~7–8 months pregnant for days/weeks)
- [ ] Nursing bra (2)
- [ ] Comfortable underwear (or use hospital mesh; bring both)
- [ ] High-waisted underwear (comfortable over cesarean incision if applicable)
- [ ] Toiletries: shampoo, conditioner, face wash, toothbrush, deodorant, moisturizer
- [ ] Phone + charging cord
- [ ] Any medications (including prenatal vitamin — hospital may not provide)
- [ ] Glasses / contacts

---

## For the Partner

- [ ] Change of clothes (stay 1–2 nights)
- [ ] Toiletries
- [ ] Snacks (cafeteria is far; partner needs to eat during long labor)
- [ ] Pillow and blanket (hospital sleeping chairs are uncomfortable)
- [ ] Phone charger
- [ ] Cash or credit card (parking, cafeteria)
- [ ] Camera (if using dedicated camera vs. phone)
- [ ] Entertainment for early/latent labor (books, tablet)
- [ ] Comfortable shoes (many hours on feet during labor support)

---

## For Baby

- [ ] Going-home outfit (bring 2 sizes: newborn and 0–3 months; size is unknown)
- [ ] Swaddle blanket
- [ ] Infant car seat (installed and inspected before leaving for hospital)
- [ ] Hat (hospital provides but bring a cute one for photos)

---

## Documents and Admin

- [ ] Insurance card
- [ ] ID (driver's license)
- [ ] Hospital pre-registration paperwork (if not done online)
- [ ] Birth plan (shared with provider already; bring copies for nursing staff)
- [ ] Pediatrician name and contact info
- [ ] Parking information (payment method)

---

## What to Leave Home

The bag can be overpacked. Items frequently noted as unnecessary:
- Full entertainment setup (labor requires focus; postpartum is spent sleeping when possible)
- Too many outfit options for baby
- Heavy books
- Jewelry or valuables

---

## After You're Admitted

Hospital provides: gown, mesh underwear, pads, ice packs, Dermoplast spray (anesthetic), stool softener, baby hat and blanket, formula samples (decline if breastfeeding). Take everything they offer — you've paid for it.
