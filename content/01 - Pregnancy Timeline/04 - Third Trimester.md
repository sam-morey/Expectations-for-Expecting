**Weeks 28–40**

The third trimester is the final stretch — baby gains most of their weight, organs finish maturing, and both mom and baby prepare for birth. Physical discomfort increases as the uterus reaches full size.

---

## What's Happening with Baby

| Week | Baby Size | Key Development |
|---|---|---|
| 28 | Eggplant | Eyes open; blinking; REM sleep; can perceive light |
| 29 | Butternut squash | Muscles and lungs maturing; gaining ~0.5 lb/week |
| 30 | Cabbage | Brain developing rapidly; lanugo (fine hair) beginning to shed |
| 31 | Coconut | Rapid brain connections; processing senses; most space now occupied |
| 32 | Jicama | Fingernails complete; toenails nearly done; practice breathing |
| 33 | Pineapple | Skull soft and flexible (for delivery); immune system developing |
| 34 | Cantaloupe | CNS and lungs nearly mature; fat plumping up skin |
| 35 | Honeydew | Kidneys fully developed; liver processing waste; good lung maturity |
| 36 | Romaine lettuce | "Early term" begins; drops into pelvis (engagement/"lightening") |
| 37 | Swiss chard | "Full term" — all systems ready for life outside womb |
| 38 | Leek | Organs fully mature; baby shedding vernix |
| 39 | Watermelon | Most common birth week; optimal readiness |
| 40 | Pumpkin | Due date; average 7–8 lbs, 19–21 inches |

## What's Happening with Mom

### Physical Changes
- **Shortness of breath** — Uterus pushing against diaphragm
- **Heartburn** — Stomach displaced upward; lower esophageal sphincter relaxed by progesterone
- **Pelvic pressure** — Baby descending; pressure on bladder and pelvis
- **Frequent urination** — Returns as baby drops and presses on bladder
- **Swelling (edema)** — Feet, ankles, hands common; sudden severe swelling may indicate preeclampsia
- **Insomnia** — Discomfort, anxiety, nocturia
- **Back and hip pain** — Relaxin loosening ligaments; pelvic girdle pain
- **Braxton Hicks** — More frequent and sometimes uncomfortable; not regular or progressive
- **Colostrum leaking** — Normal from second trimester on
- **Carpal tunnel syndrome** — Fluid retention compressing wrist nerves

### Emotional
- Anticipation, nesting instinct, and anxiety about labor typically intensify
- Birth fear is normal; birth preparation education reduces anxiety
- Feeling "done with pregnancy" in final weeks is nearly universal

## Medical Care in the Third Trimester

| Test / Event | Timing | What It Is |
|---|---|---|
| Prenatal appointments | Every 2 weeks (28–36 weeks); weekly (36–40 weeks) | Weight, BP, fundal height, presentation, heartbeat |
| Growth ultrasound | As needed; routinely ~28–32 weeks in some practices | Estimates fetal weight; checks fluid and placenta |
| Group B Strep (GBS) test | Week 35–37 | Vaginal/rectal swab; if positive, IV antibiotics during labor |
| Non-stress test (NST) | If indicated; often post-dates | Monitors fetal heart rate reactivity |
| Biophysical profile (BPP) | If NST non-reactive | NST + ultrasound; checks breathing, movement, tone, fluid |
| Cervical check | Last few weeks (provider-dependent) | Dilation and effacement; not predictive of labor onset |

## Kick Counts

After 28 weeks, kick counts monitor fetal wellbeing:
- Choose a consistent time daily (baby usually most active after meals)
- Count movements until you feel 10 in 2 hours
- Normal: 10 movements within 1–2 hours
- Contact provider if you notice a significant reduction in normal movement pattern

**Trust your instincts.** You know your baby's movement patterns better than any chart.

## Signs That Labor Is Approaching

- Lightening (baby drops lower into pelvis) — weeks before labor
- Mucus plug loss — can be days to weeks before labor
- Bloody show — tinged mucus; labor often within 24–72 hours
- Nesting instinct surge
- Loose stools / GI changes day before labor
- See [[02 - Signs of Labor]] for full detail
