Pregnancy is divided into three trimesters across approximately 40 weeks, counted from the first day of the last menstrual period (LMP). Full term is 39–40 weeks; the range 37–42 weeks is considered normal.

---

## Trimester Summary

| Trimester | Weeks | Key Events |
|---|---|---|
| First | 1–13 | Implantation; organ formation; highest miscarriage risk; morning sickness peak |
| Second | 14–27 | Growth acceleration; anatomy scan; movement felt; most comfortable trimester |
| Third | 28–40 | Rapid weight gain; lung maturation; positioning; preparation for birth |

## 40-Week Overview

### Weeks 1–4 — Early Implantation
- Ovulation and fertilization (~week 2)
- Implantation into uterine lining (~day 6–10 post-ovulation)
- hCG production begins; pregnancy test turns positive
- Embryonic disc forming; no visible features yet

### Weeks 5–8 — Organ Formation Begins
- Heart begins beating (~week 6)
- Neural tube closes (brain and spinal cord formation)
- Limb buds appear
- Most nausea and fatigue begin

### Weeks 9–13 — Fetus Stage Begins
- All major organs present, beginning to function
- Fingers and toes distinguishable
- Size grows from ~1 inch to ~3 inches
- Miscarriage risk drops significantly after week 10–12

### Weeks 14–17 — Second Trimester Begins
- Placenta fully functional (nausea often improves)
- Baby begins making facial expressions
- Skeleton hardening from cartilage to bone
- Sex potentially visible on ultrasound

### Weeks 18–22 — Anatomy Scan Window
- Anatomy scan (20-week) assesses all organs and structures
- Quickening (first movement) often felt ~weeks 18–20 (earlier in subsequent pregnancies)
- Baby can hear sounds and swallow amniotic fluid
- Vernix and lanugo (fine hair) begin forming

### Weeks 23–27 — Viability Window
- Viability threshold: ~22–24 weeks (with intensive NICU care)
- Lungs begin producing surfactant
- Baby responds to sound and light
- Rapid brain development phase begins

### Weeks 28–31 — Third Trimester Begins
- Eyes open; blinking begins
- Baby gains ~0.5 lb/week from here
- Sleep cycles established
- Braxton Hicks contractions common

### Weeks 32–35 — Finishing Development
- Lungs nearly mature
- Baby assumes head-down position (ideally)
- Fat deposits filling out
- Group B Strep test done at 35–37 weeks

### Weeks 36–40 — Full Term Preparation
- Considered "early term" at 37–38 weeks; "full term" at 39–40 weeks
- Baby drops into pelvis ("lightening")
- Cervical ripening and effacement begin
- Average birth weight: 7–8 lbs

---

## Due Date Accuracy

- Due dates are estimates based on a 28-day cycle; individual variation is normal
- Only ~5% of babies are born on their exact due date
- 80% of births occur between 38 and 42 weeks
- Induction is typically offered at 41–42 weeks if labor has not begun
