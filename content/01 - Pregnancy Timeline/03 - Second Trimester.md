# Second Trimester

**Weeks 14–27**

The second trimester is often called the "honeymoon" of pregnancy. Nausea typically subsides, energy returns, and the pregnancy becomes more visible. It's also when parents often feel the baby move for the first time.

## What's Happening with Mom

### Physical Changes
- **Round ligament pain** — Sharp pains on lower sides of abdomen as uterus expands
- **Baby bump visible** — Uterus rises above pelvic bone; showing typically around weeks 16–20
- **Quickening** — First fetal movement felt; flutters at ~18–20 weeks (earlier in subsequent pregnancies; ~16 weeks)
- **Skin changes** — Linea nigra (dark line on abdomen), melasma (facial pigmentation), stretch marks beginning
- **Back pain** — Center of gravity shifts; lumbar pressure increases
- **Leg cramps** — Common at night; often calcium/magnesium related
- **Breast growth** — Colostrum (pre-milk) may begin leaking
- **Increased appetite** — Nausea resolved; caloric needs increasing (~350 extra kcal/day second trimester)
- **Braxton Hicks** — Irregular practice contractions; normal from mid-second trimester

### Emotional
- Usually the most emotionally stable trimester
- Anxiety about anatomy scan results is common
- Fetal movement creates stronger emotional connection with baby

## Medical Care in the Second Trimester

| Test / Event | Timing | What It Is |
|---|---|---|
| Quad screen | Week 15–20 | Blood test for chromosomal and neural tube abnormalities |
| Anatomy scan (20-week ultrasound) | Week 18–22 | Comprehensive organ, spine, face, limb assessment; sex determination |
| Cervical length check | Week 16–24 | If risk factors for preterm labor |
| Glucose challenge test (GCT) | Week 24–28 | Screens for gestational diabetes |
| Glucose tolerance test (GTT) | If GCT positive | Confirms or rules out gestational diabetes |
| Prenatal appointments | Every 4 weeks | Weight, BP, fundal height, heartbeat |
| Amniocentesis (if indicated) | Week 15–20 | Diagnostic test for chromosomal abnormalities |

## Anatomy Scan (20-Week Ultrasound)

The anatomy scan is the most comprehensive ultrasound of pregnancy. It assesses:
- Brain structure (ventricles, cerebellum)
- Spine and neural tube
- Heart (four chambers, outflow tracts)
- Kidneys and bladder
- Abdominal wall
- Limbs and digits
- Face (cleft lip/palate)
- Placenta location and amniotic fluid volume
- Cervical length
- Fetal sex (if desired)

Most anatomy scans take 30–60 minutes. If findings are incomplete (baby uncooperative), a follow-up scan may be ordered.

## What to Do in the Second Trimester

- [ ] Anatomy scan completed
- [ ] Begin telling everyone if desired
- [ ] Start researching childbirth education classes
- [ ] Tour hospital / birth center
- [ ] Begin researching pediatricians
- [ ] Start thinking about birth plan
- [ ] Begin tracking fetal movements (after ~24 weeks)
- [ ] Research maternity leave and FMLA options
- [ ] Start thinking about childcare options (waitlists can be 6–18 months)
- [ ] Begin baby registry
