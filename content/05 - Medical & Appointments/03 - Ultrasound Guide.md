# Ultrasound Guide

Ultrasound is the primary imaging tool of prenatal care. It uses high-frequency sound waves (no radiation) to produce images of the developing baby, placenta, and uterine environment.

---

## Types of Ultrasound

| Type | How Done | When Used |
|---|---|---|
| Transvaginal (TV) | Internal probe; clearer early images | First trimester (weeks 6–10); better for early dating and heartbeat |
| Transabdominal | Probe on abdomen with gel | Standard from second trimester onward |
| Doppler | Auditory component; measures blood flow | Fetal heart rate assessment; umbilical cord blood flow |
| 3D/4D | Produces surface images; optional | Not diagnostic; often offered as add-on or keepsake |

---

## Ultrasounds by Trimester

### First Trimester Ultrasounds

**Dating ultrasound (weeks 7–10):**
- Confirms intrauterine pregnancy (rules out ectopic)
- Detects heartbeat (should be visible at 6–7 weeks transvaginally; may not be seen before 6 weeks)
- Establishes gestational age via crown-rump length (CRL)
- Identifies number of gestational sacs (singleton vs. twins)

**NT scan (weeks 11–14):**
- Specialized scan measuring nuchal translucency (fluid at back of neck)
- Requires NT-certified sonographer
- Combined with blood markers for Down syndrome risk assessment

### Second Trimester Ultrasound

**Anatomy scan (weeks 18–22):** The most comprehensive ultrasound in pregnancy.

**What is assessed:**
- **Head and brain:** Ventricles, cerebellum, neural tube, face (cleft lip/palate)
- **Spine:** Vertebrae alignment; neural tube defects
- **Heart:** Four chambers, outflow tracts, rhythm; many heart defects detectable
- **Abdomen:** Kidneys, bladder, stomach (should be full of swallowed fluid), abdominal wall closure
- **Extremities:** Arms, legs, hands, feet, digits
- **Placenta:** Location (is it low-lying?), appearance, attachment
- **Amniotic fluid:** Volume; too little (oligohydramnios) or too much (polyhydramnios) flagged
- **Umbilical cord:** Three-vessel cord confirmed
- **Cervical length:** Short cervix may indicate preterm labor risk
- **Fetal sex:** If parents desire; accuracy ~99% at 20 weeks with cooperative baby

**If anatomy is incomplete:** A follow-up scan is scheduled. Baby position or fetal size can obstruct views. This is common and not a cause for alarm.

### Third Trimester Ultrasound

**Growth scan (weeks 28–36, varies by practice):**
- Estimates fetal weight (±15–20% margin of error)
- Confirms fetal position (vertex/head-down vs. breech vs. transverse)
- Checks placental position and amniotic fluid index (AFI)
- Umbilical artery Doppler if growth restriction suspected

**Biophysical profile (BPP):**
- Ultrasound component of fetal wellbeing assessment
- See [[02 - Prenatal Tests & Screenings]] for detail

## Understanding Ultrasound Results

### Soft Markers
Findings that slightly increase the statistical risk of chromosomal abnormalities but are often present in normal pregnancies:
- Choroid plexus cysts (CPCs)
- Echogenic intracardiac focus (EIF)
- Mild pyelectasis (kidney dilation)
- Short long bones

**Context:** Soft markers in the presence of normal NIPT/genetic screening results are typically not clinically significant. Ask your provider what any finding means in the context of your full picture.

### Placenta Previa
- Placenta covering the cervical os (opening)
- If identified at anatomy scan: usually resolves as uterus grows (~90% resolve by term)
- If persisting at 28–32 weeks: follow-up scan ordered; may require modified activity or cesarean birth

### Amniotic Fluid Levels
- **Normal:** AFI 8–24 cm; deepest pocket ≥2 cm
- **Oligohydramnios (too little):** Kidney issues, membrane leak, post-dates; may require closer monitoring
- **Polyhydramnios (too much):** Fetal swallowing issues, diabetes, structural anomaly; increased monitoring

---

## Keepsake 3D/4D Ultrasounds

Not a diagnostic tool — purely for viewing baby's surface features. The optimal window is ~28–32 weeks (baby has fat deposits but still has space to move). These scans have no proven safety concerns but are not FDA-recommended for non-medical use.
