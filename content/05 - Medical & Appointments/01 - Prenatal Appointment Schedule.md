# Prenatal Appointment Schedule

Prenatal care follows a predictable schedule that intensifies as pregnancy progresses. The frequency of appointments reflects the increasing complexity of monitoring as delivery approaches.

---

## Standard Appointment Frequency

| Phase | Weeks | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Early pregnancy | 8–28 weeks | Every 4 weeks |
| Late second / early third | 28–36 weeks | Every 2 weeks |
| Final weeks | 36–40 weeks | Every week |
| Post-dates | 40+ weeks | Every 2–3 days with NST |

**High-risk pregnancies** (twins, gestational diabetes, preeclampsia history, advanced maternal age, etc.) may have more frequent appointments and additional monitoring.

---

## What Happens at Every Prenatal Appointment

| Check | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Weight | Track weight gain trend; sudden gain can indicate preeclampsia (fluid retention) |
| Blood pressure | Screen for preeclampsia (140/90+ is the threshold) |
| Urinalysis | Protein (preeclampsia), glucose (gestational diabetes), infection |
| Fundal height | Tape measure from pubic bone to top of uterus; tracks fetal growth (1cm ≈ 1 week after 20 weeks) |
| Fetal heartbeat | Doppler; normal range 110–160 bpm; provider listens for rate and regularity |
| Symptoms review | Any new concerns; opportunity to ask questions |

## Appointment-by-Appointment Guide

### First Appointment (~Week 8–10)
- Confirm pregnancy; establish due date
- Full medical history and physical exam
- Blood panel: blood type, Rh factor, CBC, rubella immunity, STI screen, thyroid, blood glucose
- Dating ultrasound (if not already done)
- Discuss genetic testing options
- Prescriptions: prenatal vitamins, Zofran if needed

### Week 10–12
- NIPT (non-invasive prenatal testing) if desired — blood draw; results in 1–2 weeks
- Review NIPT consent and options

### Week 11–14
- Nuchal translucency (NT) ultrasound: specialized scan + blood markers
- First trimester screening results discussion

### Week 16–20
- Routine visit; fundal height now trackable
- Quad screen blood draw (if not done NIPT)
- Anatomy scan scheduled

### Week 18–22 (Anatomy Scan)
- Full anatomy scan: all major organ systems reviewed
- Sex determination if desired
- Placenta location confirmed
- May include cervical length measurement

### Week 24–28
- Glucose challenge test (GCT) — no fasting required
- Iron levels reassessed
- Discussion of birth preferences begins

### Week 28
- If Rh-negative: RhoGAM injection
- Tdap vaccine discussion (recommended weeks 27–36)
- Begin every-2-week schedule

### Week 32–34
- Growth ultrasound (in some practices; varies)
- Fetal position assessment
- Birth plan discussion

### Week 35–37
- Group B Strep (GBS) swab — vaginal and rectal
- Cervical check may begin (provider-dependent)
- Hospital bag preparation review

### Week 36–40 (Weekly)
- Cervical dilation and effacement checks
- Fetal presentation confirmed
- Birth plan finalized
- Induction discussion at 39–41 weeks

## Questions to Ask at Appointments

**General:**
- Is my weight gain on track?
- How is baby's growth/position?
- Any concerns from today's findings?

**First trimester:**
- What genetic tests do you recommend for my situation?
- What symptoms warrant a call?

**Second trimester:**
- What did the anatomy scan show?
- When is my glucose test?

**Third trimester:**
- Is baby in position?
- What are your induction criteria/timeline?
- What are the signs of labor for my specific situation?
- When should I go to L&D vs. call first?
