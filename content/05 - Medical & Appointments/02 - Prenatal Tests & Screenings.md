Prenatal testing ranges from routine monitoring (included at every appointment) to optional genetic screening. Understanding what each test is, what it measures, and what the results mean enables informed decision-making.

---

## Screening vs. Diagnostic Tests

| Type | What It Is | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Screening** | Estimates probability of a condition; cannot confirm | NIPT, NT scan, quad screen |
| **Diagnostic** | Confirms or rules out a condition; invasive | CVS, amniocentesis |

A positive screening result means increased risk — not a diagnosis. Diagnostic testing is required to confirm.

---

## First Trimester Tests

### NIPT (Non-Invasive Prenatal Testing / Cell-Free DNA)
- **When:** Week 10+
- **What:** Blood draw analyzing fetal DNA fragments in maternal blood
- **Screens for:** Trisomy 21 (Down), Trisomy 18, Trisomy 13, sex chromosome abnormalities; optional sex determination
- **Accuracy:** 97–99% sensitivity for trisomy 21
- **Limitations:** Screening only — false positives occur; positive result should be followed by diagnostic testing
- **Cost:** May be covered by insurance; sometimes out-of-pocket $200–600

### Nuchal Translucency (NT) Scan
- **When:** Week 11–14
- **What:** Ultrasound measuring fluid at the back of baby's neck; combined with blood markers (PAPP-A, hCG)
- **Screens for:** Down syndrome, Trisomy 18, heart defects
- **Accuracy:** ~85% detection (combined screening); less sensitive than NIPT
- **Note:** Requires specialized NT-certified ultrasound technician

### CVS (Chorionic Villus Sampling)
- **When:** Week 10–13
- **What:** Diagnostic; sample of placental tissue taken via thin needle
- **Tests for:** Chromosomal abnormalities (full karyotype); confirms NIPT positive
- **Accuracy:** >99%
- **Risk:** ~0.5–1% procedure-related pregnancy loss
- **When offered:** After high-risk screening result; advanced maternal age (35+); prior affected pregnancy

## Second Trimester Tests

### Quad Screen (Maternal Serum Screening)
- **When:** Week 15–20
- **What:** Blood test measuring 4 markers (AFP, hCG, estriol, inhibin A)
- **Screens for:** Down syndrome, Trisomy 18, neural tube defects
- **Accuracy:** ~80% detection for Down syndrome; higher false positive rate than NIPT

### Anatomy Scan (20-Week Ultrasound)
- **When:** Week 18–22
- **What:** Comprehensive ultrasound assessing all major structures
- **See:** [[03 - Ultrasound Guide]] for full detail

### Amniocentesis
- **When:** Week 15–20
- **What:** Diagnostic; sample of amniotic fluid via needle under ultrasound guidance
- **Tests for:** Full chromosomal karyotype; neural tube defects; specific genetic conditions
- **Accuracy:** >99%
- **Risk:** ~0.1–0.3% procedure-related pregnancy loss (very low risk at experienced centers)
- **When offered:** High-risk NIPT; positive anatomy scan findings; advanced maternal age; personal preference for definitive diagnosis

### Glucose Challenge Test (GCT)
- **When:** Week 24–28
- **What:** Drink 50g glucose solution; blood draw 1 hour later; no fasting required
- **Screens for:** Gestational diabetes
- **Result interpretation:** <140 mg/dL normal; ≥140 mg/dL (some labs use 130) → proceed to GTT

### Glucose Tolerance Test (GTT)
- **When:** If GCT positive
- **What:** Fasting blood draw; drink 100g glucose solution; blood draws at 1, 2, 3 hours
- **Diagnoses:** Gestational diabetes (2+ values above threshold)

## Third Trimester Tests

### Group B Strep (GBS) Test
- **When:** Week 35–37
- **What:** Swab of vaginal and rectal areas; tests for GBS bacteria
- **Result:** ~25–30% of women are GBS positive — does not indicate infection or cause symptoms
- **If positive:** IV antibiotics during labor to protect newborn from transmission

### Non-Stress Test (NST)
- **When:** If post-dates (41+ weeks), gestational diabetes, reduced movement, or other risk factors
- **What:** Fetal heart rate monitoring via external monitor for 20–40 minutes; looks for heart rate acceleration with movement
- **Reactive:** Two accelerations in 20 minutes = normal/reassuring
- **Non-reactive:** May require further evaluation; not necessarily abnormal

### Biophysical Profile (BPP)
- **When:** If NST non-reactive; post-dates; high-risk
- **What:** NST + ultrasound assessing fetal breathing, movement, tone, and amniotic fluid volume
- **Score:** 8–10/10 = reassuring; <6 = concerning; may prompt delivery decision
