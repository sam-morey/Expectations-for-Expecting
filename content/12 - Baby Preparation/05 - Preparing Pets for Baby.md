Pets are often the "first babies" in a household. The arrival of a newborn can be a massive shock to their routine and territory. Preparing them early reduces stress for both the animal and the parents, and ensures a safe introduction.

---

## Early Preparation (During Pregnancy)

The goal is to desensitize your pet to the sights, sounds, and smells of a baby before the baby arrives.

### Sounds and Smells
- **Baby sounds:** Play recordings of baby crying, cooing, and babbling at a low volume while giving your pet treats. Gradually increase the volume.
- **Baby gear:** Set up the crib, swing, and stroller early so the pet can investigate and realize they aren't scary.
- **Smells:** Use baby lotion or powder on yourself so the scent becomes familiar and associated with "home."

### Routine and Boundaries
- **Establish boundaries:** If the nursery is off-limits, start closing the door or using a baby gate now.
- **Routine shift:** If your pet's walks or feeding times will change when the baby is here, start shifting to that new schedule gradually.
- **Command review:** Ensure your dog has a solid "leave it," "sit/stay," and "go to your place" command.

---

## The Introduction

How you handle the first few minutes can set the tone for the relationship.

| Step | Action | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **The Scent** | Have the partner bring home a worn baby hat or blanket from the hospital before baby comes home. | Let the pet sniff it in a calm environment. |
| **The Greeting** | When arriving home, have the partner hold the baby while the birthing parent greets the pet first (since they've been gone). | Reduces "territorial" excitement or anxiety. |
| **The Meeting** | Keep the pet on a loose leash. Allow them to sniff the baby's feet from a distance while staying calm. | Rewards calm behavior with treats/praise. |

---

## Maintaining the Bond

A common cause of pet anxiety is the sudden loss of attention.

- **Special Time:** Dedicate 10–15 minutes of undivided attention to your pet daily.
- **Positive Association:** Give the pet a special treat or a stuffed toy only when you are nursing or holding the baby. This teaches them that "baby time = good things for me."
- **Safety First:** Never, under any circumstances, leave a pet unattended with a baby, regardless of how "good" or "trusted" the pet is.
