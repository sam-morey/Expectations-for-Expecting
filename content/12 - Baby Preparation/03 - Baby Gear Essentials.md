# Baby Gear Essentials

A curated guide to what you actually need vs. what the baby industry wants you to buy. Most babies need very little — a safe place to sleep, food, warmth, and human contact.

---

## Tier 1 — Non-Negotiable

These items are needed from day 1:

| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Infant car seat** | Required to leave the hospital; install before 36 weeks; get it inspected at a certified station |
| **Safe sleep surface** (crib or bassinet) | Firm, flat, safety-certified; see [[01 - Safe Sleep Guidelines]] |
| **Fitted crib sheets** (2–3) | Bare sleep surface; nothing else in the crib |
| **Swaddle blankets** (3–4) | Muslin swaddles (Aden + Anais) or zippered swaddlers (HALO SleepSack Swaddle); reduces startle reflex |
| **Onesies and sleepers** | Newborn (5–7) + 0–3 months (10–12); babies stay in sleepers; footie pajamas easiest |
| **Feeding supplies** | Nursing bras, nipple cream, breast pads if breastfeeding; bottles and formula if not |
| **Diapers** | Small stock of newborn; order size 1 in bulk (most babies outgrow newborn quickly) |
| **Wipes** | Unscented; buy in bulk |
| **Diaper cream** | Aquaphor or Desitin for prevention and treatment |
| **Baby bathtub** | Supportive insert for newborn bathing |
| **Baby nail file or clippers** | Newborn nails grow fast; scratching is a real issue |
| **Thermometer** (rectal) | Fever check in newborns; most accurate is rectal; have one ready |
| **Bulb syringe or NoseFrida** | Nasal congestion in newborns is common; they can't blow their noses |

## Tier 2 — Highly Useful

| Item | Why Useful | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **White noise machine** | Improves sleep quality for baby and parents | Dedicated machine beats a phone app |
| **Baby carrier / wrap** | Hands-free caregiving; calms fussy babies; supports development | Learn before baby arrives; Solly Baby (wrap) or Ergobaby Embrace (structured) for newborns |
| **Bouncer or swing** | "Third arm" for settling a fussy baby; allows hands-free moments | Not a sleep surface; not for unsupervised sleep |
| **Stroller** | Needed sooner than expected for walks | See [[03 - Stroller & Car Seat Guide]] |
| **Nursing pillow** (Boppy or My Brest Friend) | Positioning support for breastfeeding; helps posture | Highly recommended for breastfeeding; useful even for bottle |
| **Burp cloths** (6–10) | You will go through many | Cloth diapers work better than thin burp cloths |
| **Pacifier** (2–3 brands) | Soothes; associated with reduced SIDS risk | Baby may refuse some brands; have options |
| **Baby monitor** | Once baby moves to own room | Audio is sufficient; video is nice but not required |
| **Sound machine / app** | White noise for sleep anywhere | |
| **Blackout curtains** | Improves daytime naps | Standard or travel version |
| **Diaper bag** | Essential for leaving the house | Backpack style recommended; test size against what you'll carry |

---

## Tier 3 — Nice to Have

| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| Baby swing (motorized) | Some babies love; some reject; borrow before buying |
| Mamaroo or high-tech soothers | Expensive; not universally effective |
| Baby food maker | Useful later (~6 months); don't need until then |
| Video baby monitor | Nice if you want to see baby; audio is sufficient for function |
| Baby gym / play mat | Useful from birth (tummy time); can wait until week 1 is survived |

---

## What to Skip

| Item | Why |
|---|---|
| Wipe warmer | Creates bacterial growth; convenience not worth it |
| Diaper Genie | Regular trash with lid works; evaluate after you see your volume |
| Crib bumpers | Safety hazard; never recommended |
| Shoes (newborn) | Babies don't walk; socks are fine |
| Elaborate nursery décor | Baby doesn't notice; invests money that won't improve outcomes |
| Multiple strollers (initially) | One versatile stroller first; assess needs later |
