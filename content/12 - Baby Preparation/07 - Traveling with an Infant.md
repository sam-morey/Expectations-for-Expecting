Traveling with an infant requires a shift from "vacation mode" to "parenting in a new location." With the right gear and realistic expectations, first trips can be successful and even enjoyable.

---

## The "Sweet Spot" for Travel

Most experts suggest waiting until the baby is at least **2–3 months old** before major travel. This ensures they have their first round of vaccines and their immune system is slightly more robust. 

**Ages 3–6 months** are often the "sweet spot" for travel: they are portable, sleep a lot, aren't mobile yet, and don't require solid food.

---

## Flying with an Infant

### Booking
- **Lap Child vs. Seat:** Babies under 2 can fly on your lap for free (or a small fee). However, the FAA recommends booking a separate seat and using a car seat for maximum safety.
- **The Bassinet:** On long-haul international flights, request a bulkhead seat with a bassinet. These are limited and must be reserved early.

### At the Airport
- **Gate Check:** You can gate-check your stroller and car seat for free on almost all airlines.
- **Liquids:** Breast milk, formula, and juice for infants are exempt from the 3.4oz liquid rule. Inform the TSA agent before screening.
- **The Carry-On:** Pack 2x the diapers/wipes you think you'll need, plus a full change of clothes for the baby AND you (in case of a "blowout").

---

## Road Trips

- **The 2-Hour Rule:** Infants should not be in a car seat for more than 2 hours at a time. Plan for frequent stops to stretch, feed, and change.
- **The Passenger Seat:** If possible, one adult should sit in the back with the baby to monitor them and provide entertainment/bottles.
- **Night Driving:** Some parents swear by driving through the night while the baby sleeps; others find the exhaustion too dangerous. Know your limits.

## Essential Travel Gear

| Item | Why |
|---|---|
| **Portable Crib (Pack 'n Play)** | Ensures a safe sleep space anywhere. |
| **White Noise Machine** | Blocks out unfamiliar hotel/Airbnb sounds. |
| **SlumberPod** | A blackout tent that fits over a travel crib (excellent for room-sharing). |
| **Baby Carrier** | Essential for navigating airports or sights where strollers won't fit. |
| **Inflatable Bath Tub** | Makes bathing in a shower-only hotel possible. |

---

## Safe Sleep on the Road

**Never** compromise on safe sleep while traveling. 
- Use a firm, flat surface (travel crib or hotel-provided crib).
- No hotel pillows, blankets, or bumpers in the crib.
- Check the crib for any recalls or loose parts if provided by a hotel.
