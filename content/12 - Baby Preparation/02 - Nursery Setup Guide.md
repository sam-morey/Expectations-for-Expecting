The nursery doesn't need to be perfect before baby arrives — but having a functional sleep and care space ready by week 36 is the goal. Function matters more than aesthetics.

---

## Nursery Essentials

### Sleep Area
- **Crib or bassinet** — Safety-certified; firm, flat mattress; fitted sheet; nothing else
- **Bassinet** (first 3–6 months): Easier for room-sharing; closer to bed for night feeds; baby may outgrow quickly
- **Crib** (longer term): Full-size lasts until 2–3 years; choose when room-sharing ends or from the start

### Feeding Area
- **Comfortable glider or rocking chair** — You will spend significant hours here; invest in comfort
- **Side table or ottoman** — For water, phone, burp cloths, nipple cream while feeding
- **Footrest** — Supports posture during long feeds

### Diaper Changing Area
- **Changing table or dresser with changing topper** — Height matters; avoid bending
- **Diaper caddy** (stocked): Diapers (size 1 and newborn), wipes, diaper cream, wet bags
- **Safety strap** — Use every time; never leave baby unattended on changing surface

### Storage
- **Dresser** — Organized by size; onesies, sleepers, bibs, hats separated
- **Hamper** — Baby laundry accumulates rapidly; one near the changing area

## Nice to Have

| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| White noise machine | Highly effective for sleep; constant womb-like sound; worth having |
| Baby monitor (audio or video) | Necessary once baby moves to their own room |
| Blackout curtains | Improves daytime naps significantly; baby's sleep is sensitive to light |
| Humidifier | Useful for dry climates; helps with congestion |
| Night light (dim, warm) | For nighttime diaper changes without full light |
| Bookshelf | Even newborns benefit from books; start early |

---

## What You Don't Need in the Nursery

| Item | Why Skip It |
|---|---|
| Bumper pads | Safety hazard; banned by AAP and recalled widely |
| Crib positioners and wedges | Safety hazard; not safe sleep |
| Baby monitors with "breathing detection" | False alarms cause anxiety; not validated |
| Diaper Genie (immediately) | Regular covered trash works; evaluate after you know your actual diaper volume |
| Wipe warmer | Convenience item; not necessary; creates bacteria if not changed regularly |

---

## Temperature and Environment

- **Room temperature:** 68–72°F (20–22°C) for sleep
- **Air quality:** Plants, air purifiers — nice additions; not essential
- **Light:** Blackout capability for naps; dim for night feeds

---

## Nursery Setup Timeline

| Timeline | Task |
|---|---|
| Weeks 28–32 | Order large items (crib, glider, dresser); shipping can take 4–8 weeks |
| Weeks 32–34 | Wash and organize baby clothing and bedding (baby detergent; no fabric softener) |
| Weeks 34–36 | Set up crib/bassinet; stock changing station; hang blackout curtains |
| Week 36 | Nursery complete; hospital bag packed |

---

## Laundry Note

Wash all baby clothing, bedding, and washcloths before use in baby-specific fragrance-free detergent (Dreft, All Free & Clear, Seventh Generation Free & Clear). Newborn skin is highly sensitive.
