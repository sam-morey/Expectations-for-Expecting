A practical guide to exercise choices at each stage, with specific modifications as the pregnancy progresses.

---

## Always Safe (All Trimesters, Modified as Needed)

| Exercise | Notes |
|---|---|
| Walking | Excellent throughout; low-impact; no modification needed |
| Swimming / water aerobics | Best third trimester option; supports weight; no impact; cooling |
| Stationary cycling | Non-impact; stable (no fall risk like outdoor cycling) |
| Prenatal yoga | Excellent for flexibility, breathing, and mental preparation; avoid deep twists and hot yoga |
| Low-impact aerobics | Modify supine positions from ~20 weeks |
| Strength training (modified) | Continue with modifications; reduce loading as needed |

---

## First Trimester (Weeks 1–13)

**General rule:** Continue all pre-pregnancy activities with minimal modification. Only add restrictions if new complications arise.

**Safe:**
- Running (if pre-existing habit; reduce if high discomfort)
- HIIT workouts (modified intensity as needed)
- Strength training at pre-pregnancy loads
- Most group fitness classes

**Modify:**
- Avoid hot yoga, hot Pilates, or sauna (temperature elevation risk during neural tube closure)
- Listen to your body — fatigue and nausea are valid reasons to reduce intensity

---

## Second Trimester (Weeks 14–27)

**Safe and ideal window:**
- **Strength training:** Maintain with form focus; reduce loads as belly grows
- **Walking and hiking:** Excellent; terrain should be manageable (no extreme balance challenges)
- **Swimming:** Excellent; low-impact with growing belly
- **Prenatal yoga/Pilates:** Ideal; builds core stability, hip flexibility, breathing
- **Cycling (stationary):** Comfortable option; outdoor cycling fine on flat terrain

**Key modifications:**
- **No flat supine (back-lying) after 16–20 weeks for >1–2 minutes** — use a wedge, prop on right hip, or switch to seated/incline positions
- **Avoid deep twists** that compress the abdomen
- **Standing balance:** Use wall or stable surface; center of gravity shifting
- **Core work:** Transition away from traditional crunches and sit-ups; diastasis recti risk with high intra-abdominal pressure; see [[03 - Pelvic Floor & Core Training]]

---

## Third Trimester (Weeks 28–40)

**Safe:**
- Walking (most important single exercise)
- Swimming and water aerobics (especially late third trimester — best relief from weight)
- Prenatal yoga
- Stationary cycling
- Modified strength training (lighter loads, stable positions)
- Pelvic floor exercises (non-negotiable throughout)

**Key modifications:**
- **Running:** Many women stop by weeks 28–32 due to pelvic pressure, but this is individual; it's safe to continue if comfortable
- **Heavy lifting:** Reduce loads; maintain form; avoid straining or Valsalva
- **Supine restriction:** Stricter after 28 weeks — always prop or modify
- **Balance:** Increased relaxin + growing belly = higher fall risk; use caution on uneven terrain, stairs, crowded spaces
- **Reduce intensity willingly:** Your workout output will be lower; this is normal physiology, not deconditioning

**Birth preparation focus (weeks 36–40):**
- Squats and lunges: Encourage fetal descent into optimal position
- Pelvic tilts
- Cat-cow (mobilizes pelvis)
- Perineal massage (begins ~weeks 34–36; not exercise, but physical preparation)
- Walking: 30+ minutes daily in final weeks supports cervical ripening

---

## What to Wear / Equipment

- Supportive sports bra (breasts are heavier throughout)
- Belly support band (reduces round ligament pain; helpful from second trimester)
- Stable, cushioned shoes (feet may swell; go up half a size)
- Water bottle always
