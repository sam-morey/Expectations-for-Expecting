# Exercise Modifications

As pregnancy progresses, specific exercises require modification to maintain safety, manage comfort, and protect the pelvic floor and abdominal wall. This is a reference for adapting common movements by trimester.

---

## General Modification Principles

1. **Temperature:** Avoid overheating — especially in the first trimester. Keep perceived exertion moderate; exercise in cool environments; hydrate well
2. **Supine position:** After ~20 weeks, avoid lying flat on your back for extended periods. Prop right hip up or switch to seated/incline positions
3. **Intra-abdominal pressure:** Avoid heavy loading that causes coning/doming of the belly midline
4. **Balance:** As the belly grows, balance is compromised — use support where needed
5. **Intensity:** Self-regulation based on talk test; decrease as pregnancy progresses naturally

## Strength Training Modifications

### Lower Body

| Exercise | Modification | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Squats | Safe throughout; reduce load in third trimester; wide stance comfortable | Hip and glute strength; birth prep |
| Lunges | Use wall support in third trimester (balance challenge) | |
| Deadlifts | Romanian deadlift or trap bar; reduce load; maintain neutral spine | Hinging pattern fine; avoid rounding under load |
| Leg press | Generally fine; avoid at maximum loads; feet wide on platform | |
| Hip thrusts | Use bench; add pad under bar; incline modification if supine uncomfortable | Excellent glute exercise throughout |

### Upper Body

| Exercise | Modification | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Overhead press | Fine throughout; seated option in third trimester for stability | |
| Bench press | Move to incline bench from ~20 weeks (supine restriction) | Dumbbell incline press works well |
| Push-ups | Move to incline push-ups (hands elevated) as belly grows; avoid coning | |
| Rows | Excellent; minimal modification; bent-over row posture supported with hand on bench | |
| Pull-ups / lat pulldown | Fine throughout | |

### Core

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|---|---|
| Crunches, sit-ups | Dead bugs, bird-dogs, Pallof press |
| Plank (if coning occurs) | Side plank, elevated plank (hands on bench), short duration |
| Double leg lowers | Single-leg heel slides |
| Heavy loaded carries with coning | Lighter farmer carry; monitor for coning |

## Cardio Modifications

### Running
- Safe throughout for experienced runners; many women continue until 30–35 weeks
- Common modifications: Reduce speed; shorter intervals; switch to run/walk alternation
- Signs to stop: Pelvic pressure/pain, leaking, pain, shortness of breath beyond normal

### Cycling (Outdoor)
- Road and trail cycling have increasing fall risk as balance shifts; many women switch to stationary by 20–25 weeks
- Stationary cycling remains excellent throughout

### HIIT
- Reduce intensity; avoid repeated jumping in second and third trimester (impact + pelvic floor pressure)
- Replace box jumps with step-ups; replace burpees with modified versions

### Swimming / Water Exercise
- No modifications needed; excellent throughout; often most comfortable in third trimester

---

## Yoga Modifications

| Modify | Reason |
|---|---|
| Avoid deep twists (compressing abdomen) | Comfort and pressure management |
| Avoid hot yoga | Temperature risk to fetus |
| Avoid pure supine poses after ~20 weeks | Vena cava compression |
| Modify downward dog (widen stance) | Belly space and balance |
| Avoid strong abdominal compression poses (full boat) | Core pressure |
| Safe throughout: cat-cow, child's pose, warrior series, supported standing poses | |

---

## When to Reduce or Stop Exercise

Listen to your body:
- If it hurts, stop
- If you're seeing coning or doming in the belly midline, modify
- If you're leaking urine, lighten the load and see a pelvic floor PT
- If Braxton Hicks persist after stopping, rest and hydrate

The goal is not to maintain peak fitness — it is to maintain function, support the pregnancy, and protect postpartum recovery.
