# Must-Have vs. Skip List

A curated reference for what's worth buying, what to borrow or buy used, and what to skip entirely.

---

## Buy New — Non-Negotiable

These items should always be purchased new:

| Item | Why New |
|---|---|
| Infant car seat | Safety item; history unknown for secondhand; may be expired or in prior accident |
| Crib mattress | Hygiene and safety; firmness may have degraded |
| Breast pump | Hygiene; motor degrades with use; insurance typically covers new |
| Thermometer | Precision critical for newborn fever detection |

---

## Must Have (Worth Every Dollar)

| Item | Why |
|---|---|
| Quality car seat | Safety is non-negotiable |
| Ergonomic baby carrier | Hands-free; calms babies; essential for getting anything done |
| White noise machine (dedicated) | Dramatically improves infant sleep |
| Good nursing pillow | If breastfeeding, your back will thank you for 100+ hours |
| Muslin swaddle blankets (Aden + Anais) | Multi-use; breathable; most babies respond well to swaddling |
| NoseFrida nasal aspirator | Works dramatically better than bulb syringe |
| Boppy or Brest Friend | Whether breastfeeding or not; positioning support |
| Rocker/glider | You will spend many hours in this; buy for comfort |
| Blackout curtains | Naps improve significantly; worth the investment |

---

## Try Before You Buy (Borrow or Buy Used First)

These items vary widely by baby:

| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| Swing (motorized) | Some babies love; some reject; $150–300; borrow first |
| Bouncer (Mamaroo, Snoo) | Snoo is $1,600 — try renting ($16–24/month) before purchasing |
| Baby carrier type | Different babies and bodies prefer different carriers; borrow multiple to find fit |
| Bottles | Baby may have strong preferences; buy 1–2 of 2–3 brands before buying 8 of one |
| Pacifier brands | Try 2–3 brands; babies are particular |

---

## Skip — Not Worth It

| Item | Why to Skip |
|---|---|
| Wipe warmer | Bacterial growth; more maintenance than value |
| Crib bumpers | Safety hazard; AAP advises against; recalled widely |
| Shoes (newborn) | Babies don't walk; socks are sufficient |
| Baby food maker (initially) | Not needed until 4–6 months; regular blender works |
| Diaper Genie | Regular trash with a lid works; less specialized plastic waste |
| Baby Einstein videos (as stimulation) | No evidence of benefit; AAP recommends no screens before 18–24 months |
| Nursing cover (for most) | Many mothers find them unnecessary; try a loose top or blanket first |
| Bath thermometer (separate) | Your elbow is accurate enough; wrist works |
| Sterilizers and bottle warmers | Dishwasher + microwave work; simpler |
| Excessive clothing in NB size | Most babies only wear newborn for 2–4 weeks; 5–7 outfits enough |

---

## Buy Used — Smart Savings

These items are safe to buy secondhand:

| Item | What to Check |
|---|---|
| Stroller | Check wheels, frame, recline mechanism; recall history |
| Baby clothing | Wash before use; inspect for wear |
| Bassinet (without mattress) | Buy new mattress; check for recalls |
| Baby carrier | No structural damage; no recalls; check harness condition |
| Play mat / activity gym | Clean well; check for broken parts |
| Bottles (glass) | Inspect thoroughly; replace nipples |
| Books | Always great used |

**Always check recalls** at cpsc.gov before using any secondhand baby gear.
