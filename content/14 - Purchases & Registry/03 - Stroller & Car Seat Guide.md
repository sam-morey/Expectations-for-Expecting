# Stroller & Car Seat Guide

Two of the biggest purchase decisions in baby gear — and the two that most require research before buying. Compatibility, lifestyle, and safety criteria all matter.

---

## Car Seat First

Choose your car seat before your stroller. Many strollers are compatible with specific infant car seat brands (allowing the seat to click directly onto the stroller without removing baby). This travel system approach is convenient and commonly used.

**See [[04 - Car Seat Safety]] for full safety guidance.**

---

## Stroller Categories

### Full-Size Stroller
- **Best for:** Daily use; neighborhood walks; city use
- **Weight:** 15–30 lbs
- **Pros:** Durable; larger canopy; more storage; better for longer use
- **Examples:** UPPAbaby Vista, Bugaboo Fox, Nuna Mixx

### Lightweight / Umbrella Stroller
- **Best for:** Travel; compact storage; second stroller
- **Weight:** 10–20 lbs
- **Pros:** Easy to fold; fits overhead bins; compact
- **Cons:** Less infant support; smaller canopy; less storage
- **Examples:** Babyzen YOYO, GB Pockit, Summer Infant 3D Lite
- **When:** Not suitable for newborns without a newborn kit; best from ~6 months

### Travel System
- **What it is:** Stroller sold with or compatible with a specific infant car seat
- **Pros:** Car seat clicks directly in; transfer sleeping baby without disturbing
- **Examples:** UPPAbaby Cruz + Mesa, Nuna Pipa + Mixx, Chicco Bravo + KeyFit 30
- **Note:** Travel system compatibility should drive the stroller/car seat decision if you want this setup

### Jogging Stroller
- **Best for:** Runners
- **Weight:** 20–30 lbs
- **Requirements:** Fixed front wheel for running; most require baby to be 6+ months (adequate head/neck control)
- **Examples:** Bob Revolution, Thule Urban Glide
- **Note:** Can substitute as a primary stroller; heavier; harder to maneuver at low speeds

### Double Stroller
- **Best for:** Twins or second baby within 3–4 years
- **Options:** Side-by-side (wider; equal experience) vs. tandem (front/back; narrower)
- **Examples:** UPPAbaby Vista (doubles with second seat), Bugaboo Donkey, Baby Jogger City Mini GT2 Double

## What to Look For

| Feature | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Recline (fully flat) | Newborns must lie flat; not all strollers recline fully without a bassinet attachment |
| One-hand fold | Essential with a baby in arms |
| Weight | You will lift this into a car trunk hundreds of times |
| Wheel type (air vs. foam) | Air wheels handle rough terrain better; foam never goes flat |
| Canopy size | Larger = more sun and rain protection |
| Handlebar height | Matters for tall parents; some are adjustable |
| Basket size | You'll use this constantly; bigger is better |
| Compatibility | Car seat; bassinet; second seat (if planning another child) |

## Decision Framework

**If you want convenience and flexibility:** Infant car seat + compatible full-size stroller (travel system)

**If you want longevity and fewer transitions:** Convertible car seat + full-size stroller with newborn-compatible bassinet attachment

**If you're in a dense city (public transit, small apartment):** Lightweight stroller + compatible infant seat; consider whether a stroller is needed at all vs. carrier for first months

**If you run:** Jogging stroller + standard infant seat; use carrier until 6 months for runs
