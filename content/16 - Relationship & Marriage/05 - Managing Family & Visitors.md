The arrival of a baby is a joyful event for family and friends, but it occurs when you are at your most vulnerable. Setting clear boundaries early protects your peace, your recovery, and your bonding time.

---

## The "Postpartum Bubble"

The first 2–6 weeks are often called the "golden month" or the "bubble." This time is for physical healing, establishing feeding, and bonding as a new family unit. You do not owe anyone access to your baby during this time.

### Discussion Points for Partners
- **Who** is allowed to visit in the first week? The first month?
- **How long** can visits last? (e.g., "1-hour maximum")
- **What help** do we actually need? (e.g., "Bring food, don't just hold the baby")
- **Who communicates** the rules? (Rule of thumb: Each partner manages their own family).

## Sample Boundary Templates

| Situation | What to Say (The Script) |
|---|---|
| **Early Visitors** | "We are so excited to introduce you to [Name]! We've decided to take the first two weeks for ourselves to recover and bond. We'll reach out when we're ready for visitors." |
| **The "Helpful" Guest** | "We'd love to have you over! Since we're still adjusting, we aren't quite up for hosting, but if you'd like to bring dinner and help with a load of laundry while you're here, that would be amazing." |
| **Unsolicited Advice** | "Thanks for sharing what worked for you! We're following the advice of our pediatrician/midwife for now, but I'll keep that in mind." |
| **Kissing the Baby** | "We're being extra careful about germs right now, so we're asking everyone to wash their hands and please don't kiss the baby's face or hands." |

## Health and Safety Rules

Setting these expectations *before* birth reduces conflict later.

- **Vaccinations:** Decide if you require visitors to have Tdap, Flu, or COVID boosters.
- **Hand Washing:** A non-negotiable for anyone holding a newborn.
- **No Sick Guests:** "If you have even a scratchy throat, please stay home and we'll FaceTime instead."
- **The "Pass Back" Rule:** If the baby cries, they go back to the parent immediately.

---

## Moving from Couple to Family

Setting boundaries with your parents (the new grandparents) is the first step in establishing your own nuclear family's values. It can be hard, but it is necessary for your long-term relationship health.

- **United Front:** You and your partner must agree on the rules and support each other when a family member pushes back.
- **It's Not Personal:** Remind family that these rules are about the baby's health and your recovery, not about keeping them away.
