Nutritional priorities shift across the three trimesters to match the most critical phases of fetal development and maternal change.

---

## First Trimester Nutrition (Weeks 1–13)

### Focus: Folate, Protein, Getting Through Nausea

**Caloric needs:** Minimal increase (0–100 extra kcal/day)  
**Biggest challenge:** Nausea and food aversions

**Priorities:**
- **Folate:** Neural tube is closing in weeks 3–4. Prenatal vitamin + folate-rich foods are critical.
- **Protein:** Aim for 70–80g/day even when eating is difficult; eggs, Greek yogurt, and smoothies are tolerable options
- **Avoid the avoidance spiral:** Eat what you can tolerate; don't stress about first trimester diet perfection
- **Prenatal vitamin:** Take consistently — even if only tolerated at night with food

**Nausea-friendly protein options:**
- Greek yogurt / cottage cheese (cold; neutral smell)
- Cheese and crackers
- Nut butter on toast
- Protein smoothies with neutral protein powder
- Eggs (if tolerated; often one of the first foods to become aversive)

**What shifts at the end of the first trimester:** As the placenta takes over hormone production around weeks 12–14, nausea improves and appetite returns.

---

## Second Trimester Nutrition (Weeks 14–27)

### Focus: Iron, Calcium, Omega-3, Caloric Increase

**Caloric needs:** +340 kcal/day  
**Biggest opportunity:** Appetite returned; best window to optimize nutrient intake

**Priorities:**
- **Iron:** Blood volume is expanding; iron needs are highest this trimester. Red meat 2–3x/week; fortified cereals; dark greens + vitamin C
- **Calcium:** Fetal skeleton hardening. 1,000mg/day total. Dairy, fortified alternatives, sardines
- **DHA (omega-3):** Brain development acceleration. 2–3 servings of low-mercury fatty fish/week or daily supplement
- **Protein increase:** 80–100g/day as fetal growth accelerates
- **Choline:** Egg yolks daily; critical but often overlooked

**Practical approach:** A second trimester eating pattern — 3 meals + 2 snacks — with protein anchoring each meal covers most nutrient needs.

**Sample high-nutrient day:**
- Breakfast: Eggs + spinach scramble + whole grain toast (protein, choline, folate, iron)
- Snack: Greek yogurt + berries (protein, calcium, vitamin C)
- Lunch: Salmon over greens + olive oil + lemon (DHA, iron, vitamin C, D)
- Snack: Cheese + apple (calcium, fiber)
- Dinner: Chicken thighs + lentils + roasted broccoli (protein, iron, folate, calcium)

---

## Third Trimester Nutrition (Weeks 28–40)

### Focus: Calories, Brain Development, Birth Preparation

**Caloric needs:** +450 kcal/day  
**Biggest challenge:** Heartburn and discomfort from full stomach; harder to eat large meals

**Priorities:**
- **Calories:** Baby gaining 0.5 lb/week; maternal fat stores building; sustained energy important
- **DHA:** Brain development is still accelerating in the third trimester
- **Iron:** Iron deficiency anemia risk highest; fatigue is a signal to check levels
- **Magnesium:** Helps with leg cramps, Braxton Hicks, sleep disruption
- **Hydration:** Especially important; dehydration worsens Braxton Hicks and fatigue

**Eating with third trimester discomfort:**
- Smaller, more frequent meals (5–6 small meals vs. 3 large)
- Upright after eating; avoid lying down for 30–60 minutes
- Avoid spicy foods, citrus, and large amounts of fat if heartburn is severe
- Cold foods and smoothies often more tolerable than hot meals when stomach space is limited

**Third trimester nutrient additions:**
- **Vitamin K2:** Fetal bone and blood clotting support; fermented foods, egg yolks, some cheeses
- **Red raspberry leaf tea:** Some midwives recommend in the last month; discuss with provider
