# Foods to Avoid During Pregnancy

Certain foods pose genuine risks during pregnancy — from foodborne illness (dangerous because immune function is modified in pregnancy), mercury toxicity, or direct fetal harm. This is a practical reference, not a reason for anxiety about every meal.

---

## High-Risk Foods to Avoid

### Raw or Undercooked Animal Products

| Food | Risk | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Raw fish / sushi (raw) | Listeria, Salmonella, parasites | Pregnant women are 10x more susceptible to Listeria; can cause miscarriage, stillbirth |
| Undercooked meat | Toxoplasma, Salmonella, E. coli | Cook all meat to safe internal temperatures |
| Raw or undercooked eggs | Salmonella | Includes runny yolks, hollandaise, raw cookie dough |
| Unpasteurized dairy (raw milk, some soft cheeses) | Listeria | Most retail dairy in the US is pasteurized; check labels |
| Deli meats (cold cuts, hot dogs) | Listeria | Bacteria can survive refrigeration; heat to steaming before eating |
| Raw sprouts | Salmonella, E. coli | High contamination rate; hard to wash off |

**Safer versions:**
- Sushi: Cooked rolls, California rolls, shrimp, vegetable rolls are fine
- Soft cheeses: Fine if made from pasteurized milk (most US cheese is; check label)
- Deli meats: Heat until steaming (165°F) to kill Listeria

### High-Mercury Fish

Mercury accumulates in large, long-lived fish and is a neurotoxin that crosses the placenta.

**Avoid entirely:**
- Swordfish
- Shark
- King mackerel
- Tilefish (Gulf of Mexico)
- Bigeye tuna

**Limit to 1 serving/week:**
- Albacore tuna (canned white tuna)
- Halibut
- Sea bass
- Grouper

**Safe to eat 2–3 servings/week (high omega-3, low mercury):**
- Salmon
- Sardines
- Trout
- Anchovies
- Shrimp
- Tilapia
- Canned light tuna (different from albacore)

## Alcohol

**No safe amount of alcohol during pregnancy has been established.** Alcohol crosses the placenta freely and can cause:
- Fetal alcohol spectrum disorders (FASDs) — range from mild to severe cognitive and behavioral effects
- Miscarriage (elevated risk with regular alcohol use)
- Stillbirth
- Preterm birth
- Birth defects

**Recommendation:** Avoid alcohol entirely throughout pregnancy.

---

## Caffeine

- **Limit to ≤200mg/day** (ACOG recommendation)
- ~200mg = 1 medium (12 oz) drip coffee, or 2 espresso shots, or 3–4 cups of tea
- Higher intake associated with small increased risk of miscarriage and low birth weight
- Occasional modest overage on a single day is not cause for alarm

**Caffeine content reference:**
| Beverage | Caffeine |
|---|---|
| 8 oz drip coffee | 95–165mg |
| Espresso (1 shot) | 63mg |
| Green tea (8 oz) | 25–45mg |
| Black tea (8 oz) | 45–70mg |
| Soda (12 oz) | 30–55mg |

## Herbal Supplements and Teas

Not regulated for safety in pregnancy. Some have known risks:

**Avoid:**
- Black cohosh (stimulates uterine contractions)
- Blue cohosh
- Pennyroyal
- High-dose herbal concentrates (even "natural" herbs)

**Generally considered safe (in normal amounts):**
- Ginger tea (excellent for nausea)
- Peppermint tea
- Chamomile tea (occasional)
- Red raspberry leaf (often used in third trimester; discuss with provider)

---

## Food Preparation and Safety Tips

- Wash all produce thoroughly (especially leafy greens and berries)
- Use separate cutting boards for raw meat and produce
- Refrigerate leftovers promptly (no longer than 2 hours at room temperature)
- Reheat all leftovers to steaming
- Wash hands before and after handling raw meat
- When in doubt about food safety, don't eat it
