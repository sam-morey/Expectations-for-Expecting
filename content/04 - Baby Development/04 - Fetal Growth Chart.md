## Ultrasound Measurements

Providers use standardized ultrasound measurements to estimate gestational age and fetal weight:

| Measurement | Abbreviation | What It Measures |
|---|---|---|
| Crown-Rump Length | CRL | Head to rump; used in first trimester for dating |
| Biparietal Diameter | BPD | Width of the skull; correlates to gestational age |
| Head Circumference | HC | Total skull size |
| Abdominal Circumference | AC | Abdominal girth; sensitive to growth restriction |
| Femur Length | FL | Thigh bone length |
| Estimated Fetal Weight | EFW | Calculated from BPD + HC + AC + FL |

**Ultrasound weight estimates have a margin of error of ±15–20%.** A baby measuring "8 lbs" on a 38-week ultrasound may be 6.5 or 9.5 lbs at birth. This is expected, not a testing error.

## Growth Percentiles

Fetal growth is plotted on a curve. "Normal" is a range:
- **10th–90th percentile:** Normal growth
- **<10th percentile (SGA):** Small for gestational age — may trigger additional monitoring
- **>90th percentile (LGA):** Large for gestational age — associated with gestational diabetes; may affect delivery planning

A single measurement is less meaningful than the growth trajectory over multiple appointments.
