# Fetal Development Overview

From a single cell to a fully formed human being in 40 weeks — fetal development follows a precise, orchestrated sequence. Understanding the stages provides context for why timing, nutrition, and exposure avoidance matter when they do.

---

## The Three Developmental Phases

### Phase 1: The Zygote / Blastocyst (Weeks 1–2)
- Fertilization of egg by sperm → zygote
- Cell division begins (mitosis) as the zygote travels down the fallopian tube
- Becomes a blastocyst (~100 cells) by day 5
- Implants in uterine lining (~days 6–10)
- The inner cell mass becomes the embryo; the outer layer becomes the placenta

### Phase 2: The Embryonic Period (Weeks 3–8)
This is the most critical developmental window — all major organ systems are established.

| Week | Landmark Development |
|---|---|
| 3 | Neural plate forming (future brain + spinal cord); heart cells present |
| 4 | Heart beating; neural tube closing; arm/leg buds beginning |
| 5 | Brain divides into five regions; liver and pancreas forming |
| 6 | Hand plates present; face forming; heart fully chambered |
| 7 | All essential organs present; rapid brain growth; lens of eye forming |
| 8 | All organs present; fingers forming; embryo moves to "fetus" designation |

**Critical window note:** Exposure to teratogens (alcohol, certain medications, infection, radiation) during weeks 3–8 carries the highest risk of structural birth defects. This is before many women know they're pregnant.

### Phase 3: The Fetal Period (Weeks 9–40)
The basic structure is in place — now it's about growth, refinement, and maturation.

| Period | Focus |
|---|---|
| Weeks 9–13 | Organ refinement; rapid growth; face features defined |
| Weeks 14–27 | Sensory development; rapid growth; viability milestone (24 weeks) |
| Weeks 28–40 | Brain development; lung maturation; fat deposition; birth preparation |

---

## Organ System Development Timeline

| System | When Formed | When Functional | When Mature |
|---|---|---|---|
| Heart | Week 3–4 | Week 6 (beating) | Month 6–7 |
| Brain/CNS | Week 3–4 | Ongoing | Continues years after birth |
| Lungs | Week 4 | Week 24 (minimal) | Week 35–36 |
| Kidneys | Week 5 | Week 10 (producing urine) | Week 32 |
| Gut | Week 5 | Week 12 (practice swallowing) | Birth |
| Eyes | Week 4–6 | Week 26 (eyes open) | Postpartum |
| Ears | Week 6 | Week 18 (hearing) | Full at birth |

---

## Fetal Growth Summary

| Trimester | Length | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| End of First (13 weeks) | ~3 inches (7.5cm) | ~1 oz (28g) |
| End of Second (27 weeks) | ~14.5 inches (37cm) | ~2 lbs (900g) |
| End of Third (40 weeks) | ~20 inches (51cm) | ~7.5 lbs (3.4kg) |

Growth is not linear — most weight gain occurs in the third trimester (baby doubles in weight between weeks 27–40).

---

## What Baby Can Sense

| Sense | When Begins | Details |
|---|---|---|
| Touch | Week 8 | Reflexes; by week 16 can feel entire body |
| Taste | Week 13–15 | Amniotic fluid carries flavor signals; baby swallows |
| Hearing | Week 18 | Responds to voice and external sounds |
| Vision | Week 26 | Eyes open; responds to light through the abdomen |
| Smell | Week 28 | Olfactory neurons functional |
