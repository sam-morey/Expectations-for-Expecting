# Fetal Movement & Kicks

Feeling your baby move is one of the most profound experiences of pregnancy — and also an important indicator of fetal wellbeing. Understanding what's normal helps parents know when to relax and when to call.

---

## When Movement Starts

| Stage | Movement Details |
|---|---|
| Week 8–10 | Baby begins moving, but movements are random and tiny — not felt |
| Week 16–20 | **Quickening** — first movement felt by mom; described as flutters, bubbles, or faint taps |
| First pregnancy | Typically felt ~18–20 weeks |
| Second+ pregnancy | Often felt as early as 16–18 weeks (body recognizes sensation) |
| Partner feeling movement | From outside: typically around weeks 24–26 |

---

## What Fetal Movement Feels Like

Movement is described differently at different stages:

| Stage | What It Feels Like |
|---|---|
| 16–20 weeks | Flutters, bubbles, butterflies — easy to confuse with gas |
| 20–28 weeks | Distinct taps, kicks, rolls — unmistakable |
| 28–34 weeks | Strong kicks, jabs, rolling — sometimes visible from outside |
| 35–40 weeks | Movements may feel different (less somersaults, more stretching and pushing) as space decreases |

**Movement type changes in late pregnancy, but frequency should not decrease.** Movements becoming stronger and harder to distinguish as rolls vs. kicks is normal. Frequency remaining steady is what matters.

---

## Kick Counts

After 28 weeks, kick counts are a simple, evidence-based tool for monitoring fetal wellbeing.

### The Count-to-10 Method
1. Choose a consistent time each day when baby is usually active (often after meals)
2. Sit or lie on your left side in a comfortable position
3. Count movements until you reach **10 movements**
4. Normal: 10 movements within 2 hours (most active babies: within 30 minutes)

**What counts as a movement:** Kicks, rolls, flutters, jabs, hiccups. A cluster of movements counts as one.

### If You Count Fewer Than 10 in 2 Hours
- Drink something cold and sweet; lie on your left side
- Count for another 1–2 hours
- If still <10 movements in 4 hours total: **call your provider**

---

## Daily Movement Patterns

- Babies have sleep cycles (~20–40 minutes) — some inactivity is normal
- Most babies are most active in the evening (late afternoon/night)
- Movement is often triggered by food, cold drinks, music, or pressure on the abdomen
- Quiet periods of 30–60 minutes are normal
- Extended quiet periods >4 hours during normally active times: worth monitoring

---

## When Movement Concerns Need Action

**Call your provider:**
- Baby hasn't moved as much as usual and cold drink/lying down doesn't change it
- Less than 10 movements in 2 hours of counting (after 28 weeks)
- You can't remember the last time you felt movement

**Do not rely on home Doppler for reassurance** — hearing a heartbeat does not confirm adequate fetal movement or wellbeing. A non-stress test (NST) at the hospital is the appropriate tool.

**Do not wait until morning if concerned at night.** L&D triage is open 24 hours.

---

## Hiccups

Fetal hiccups are common and normal from second trimester onward. They're caused by the diaphragm contracting as baby practices breathing movements. You'll feel rhythmic, repetitive taps at a regular interval.

---

## What Strong Kicks Mean

Strong kicks are not dangerous to baby. The inside of the uterus is a padded, fluid-filled environment. Baby can kick hard without harming themselves. Rib kicks are uncomfortable for mom but fine for baby.
