While the physical recovery belongs to the birthing parent, the postpartum transition affects both partners. Partners are susceptible to sleep deprivation, stress, and their own form of postpartum mood disorders. A healthy partner is essential for a healthy family.

---

## The Partner's Role: The Protector

In the first few weeks, the partner's primary job is to "protect the bubble."

- **Gatekeeper:** Manage the visitors, the phone calls, and the front door.
- **Provider:** Ensure the birthing parent is fed, hydrated, and has what they need (pillows, water, snacks) during feedings.
- **Logistics Lead:** Take over the household chores — laundry, dishes, meal prep, and pet care — entirely for at least the first 2 weeks.

---

## Partner Mental Health

**Paternal Postpartum Depression (PPPD)** is real. It affects ~10% of partners and can manifest differently than maternal depression.

| Symptom | What it Looks Like |
|---|---|
| **Irritability** | "Short fuse," anger, or snapping at others. |
| **Withdrawal** | Working more, spending more time on a phone, or disengaging from the family. |
| **Physical Aches** | Headaches or stomach issues without a clear cause. |
| **Anxiety** | Constant worry about finances, safety, or "doing it wrong." |

**Action:** If these symptoms last more than two weeks, speak to a therapist or your primary care doctor. See [[02 - Postpartum Mental Health]] for more context.

---

## Supporting Bonding

Partners often feel "second best" in the early days, especially if the baby is breastfeeding. You are not a bystander.

- **Skin-to-Skin:** Holding the baby shirtless is just as beneficial for partners as it is for birthing parents. It releases oxytocin and helps regulate the baby's temperature and heart rate.
- **The "Soothe" Expert:** Become the person who can settle the baby between feeds. Learn the "5 S's" (Swaddle, Side/Stomach, Shush, Swing, Suck).
- **Diaper Duty:** Make this your dedicated time. It’s a recurring chance for one-on-one interaction and "serving" your new family.

---

## Relationship Maintenance

The transition to parenthood is a major stressor for the relationship.

- **Check-ins:** Once a day, ask: "How are you doing?" and "What is one thing I can do to help you right now?"
- **Low Expectations:** Accept that the house will be messy and meals will be simple. Prioritize sleep and kindness over productivity.
