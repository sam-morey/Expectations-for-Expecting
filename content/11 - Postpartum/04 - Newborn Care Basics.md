# Newborn Care Basics

A practical reference for the first weeks with a newborn. Everything is learnable. Confidence comes from doing, not from reading.

---

## Normal Newborn Behavior

Understanding what's normal prevents unnecessary panic:

| Behavior | Why |
|---|---|
| Sleeping 16–20 hours/day | Normal; brain development occurs during sleep |
| Waking every 1.5–3 hours | Stomach size of a marble → Day 1; needs frequent small feeds |
| Crying as only communication | No words; crying = hunger, tiredness, overstimulation, discomfort, or need for contact |
| Startling (Moro reflex) | Normal; startle reflex from sudden sound or movement; swaddling reduces this |
| Crossed eyes | Normal until ~3–4 months; eye muscles strengthening |
| Irregular breathing | Brief pauses in breathing are normal in newborns; concerning if prolonged |
| Grunting and straining | Normal during bowel movements; newborn gut is learning |
| Hiccups | Normal; very common; not distressing to baby |
| Skin changes (jaundice, peeling, milia) | See below |

## Feeding

### Newborn Feeding Schedule
- **Breastfed:** 8–12 times per 24 hours; every 2–3 hours; don't wait more than 3 hours in the first weeks
- **Formula fed:** 2–3 oz every 2–3 hours initially; adjust based on hunger cues
- **Feed on demand** — hunger cues (rooting, sucking fists, turning head) vs. hunger cries (latest signal)

### Is Baby Getting Enough?
Track wet and dirty diapers as the best proxy:
- Day 1: 1 wet diaper; 1 dark meconium poop
- Day 2: 2 wet; dark poop
- Day 3–5: 3–4 wet diapers; color transitioning to yellow
- Day 5+: 6+ wet diapers/day; yellow seedy poops (breastfed); yellow/brown (formula)

Weight loss of up to 7–10% of birth weight in the first days is normal; should regain to birth weight by days 10–14.

## Umbilical Cord Care

- Keep the stump dry and clean
- Allow to fall off naturally (7–21 days)
- Fold diaper below it to avoid irritation
- Sponge bathe only until it falls off
- Normal: Dries, shrivels, turns black, falls off
- Contact provider: Bleeding, foul smell, redness spreading onto the skin, oozing

---

## Bathing

- Sponge baths until umbilical cord falls off (~2 weeks)
- Newborns do not need daily baths — 2–3 times per week is sufficient
- Use warm (not hot) water; no soap needed on face; gentle fragrance-free wash on body
- Never leave baby unattended in water

---

## Skin Conditions

| Condition | What It Is | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Peeling skin | Normal; vernix drying off | No treatment needed |
| Milia | Small white bumps on nose/face | Normal; disappear on their own |
| Baby acne | Red pimples appearing weeks 2–6 | Normal; no treatment; resolves by ~month 3 |
| Jaundice (yellow tinge) | Bilirubin buildup from red blood cell breakdown | Normal if mild; provider checks bilirubin level; severe jaundice requires phototherapy |
| Erythema toxicum | Blotchy red rash with small yellow centers | Normal; no treatment |

## Sleep Environment

Safe sleep every time:
- **Always on their back**
- **Firm, flat surface** (no inclined sleepers — these have caused deaths)
- **Bare crib** (no bumpers, pillows, stuffed animals, loose blankets)
- **Own sleep surface** (AAP recommends room-sharing but not bed-sharing)
- **Temperature:** Room at 68–72°F; dress in one extra layer from what you'd wear comfortably

See [[01 - Safe Sleep Guidelines]] for full detail.

## Soothing a Crying Newborn

Dr. Harvey Karp's "5 S's" framework:
1. **Swaddle** — Tight swaddle mimics womb containment
2. **Side or stomach position** (while awake and held; never for sleep)
3. **Shush** — White noise at the volume of a shower
4. **Swing** — Rhythmic, gentle side-to-side movement
5. **Suck** — Pacifier; nursing

Working through these systematically resolves most infant crying. If all 5 fail and baby has been recently fed, check for diaper, temperature, hair tourniquet (hair wrapped around a finger/toe).

## When to Call the Pediatrician

- Fever >100.4°F rectal in first 3 months → go to ER immediately
- Inconsolable crying for >2 hours
- Won't eat; refuses multiple feeds
- No wet diapers for >8 hours
- Signs of dehydration (sunken fontanelle, no tears)
- Labored breathing; lips or fingernails turning blue
- Jaundice spreading to limbs or eyes looking yellow
- Any concern you cannot resolve
