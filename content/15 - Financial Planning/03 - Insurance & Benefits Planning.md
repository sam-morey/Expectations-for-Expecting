# Insurance & Benefits Planning

Understanding your insurance and employment benefits before the baby arrives prevents expensive surprises and ensures you capture everything you're entitled to.

---

## Health Insurance

### Pregnancy Coverage
- Prenatal care is covered as preventive care under the ACA — typically no cost-sharing for routine prenatal visits
- Labor and delivery: Subject to your deductible and out-of-pocket maximum
- **Know your out-of-pocket maximum** — delivery can cost $10,000–$30,000 billed; what you pay depends entirely on your plan

### Hospital Selection
- Verify your hospital and OB are in-network before choosing
- Out-of-network birth can result in massive unexpected bills even if the OB is in-network (anesthesiologist may be out-of-network)
- Review surprise billing protections under the No Surprises Act (2022)

### Adding Baby to Insurance
- **Critical deadline: 30 days from birth** to add baby to health insurance
- Failure to add within 30 days = may have to wait until open enrollment or next qualifying life event
- Contact HR or insurance provider within the first week after birth
- Baby's first well visits, vaccines, and any NICU care all require coverage from day 1

## Life Insurance

Having a child is one of the strongest reasons to review life insurance.

**If you don't have life insurance:**
- Term life insurance is the most cost-effective choice for most families
- 20–30 year term; coverage amount = 10–12x gross annual income per earning partner
- Apply during pregnancy (rates are based on health; healthier = lower rates)

**If you already have life insurance:**
- Review coverage amount; pre-child amounts may be insufficient
- Consider increasing coverage if income has grown

---

## Disability Insurance

Often overlooked but critical.

- Short-term disability (STD): Often covers 6–12 weeks of maternity leave at 60–70% salary; check your employer's policy
- Long-term disability (LTD): Income replacement if you're unable to work for >3–6 months; many employer policies exist but coverage amounts vary

**Review STD enrollment timing:** Some plans have waiting periods. You must be enrolled before becoming pregnant to claim maternity-related disability.

## Leave Benefits

### FMLA (Federal Family Medical Leave Act)
- 12 weeks of **unpaid**, job-protected leave per year
- Applies to companies with 50+ employees; employee must have worked there 12+ months
- Both parents eligible; can be taken simultaneously or at different times
- Does not provide pay; but protects job

### State Paid Leave
Many states offer paid family leave (PFL) programs. Check your state:

| State | Program | Benefit |
|---|---|---|
| California | CA-PFL | Up to 60–70% wage; 8 weeks |
| New York | NY PFL | Up to 67% wage; 12 weeks |
| Washington | WA PFML | Up to 90% wage (lower incomes); 12 weeks |
| Massachusetts | MAPFML | Up to 80% wage; 12 weeks |
| New Jersey | NJ TDI | Up to 85% wage; 12 weeks |
| Colorado, Connecticut, Oregon, others | State programs | Varies |

### Employer Leave
- Review your employer's specific parental leave policy in your HR handbook
- Some employers offer more generous paid leave than required by state
- Understand the distinction between "maternity leave" (just birthing parent) and "parental leave" (both parents)

---

## Tax Considerations

| Benefit | Details |
|---|---|
| Dependent Care FSA | Up to $5,000/year pre-tax for qualifying childcare |
| Child Tax Credit | $2,000/child under 17 (check current law) |
| Child and Dependent Care Tax Credit | Alternative to DC-FSA; usually less valuable for higher earners |
| 529 Contributions | No federal deduction; some state deductions; tax-free growth for education |

## Action Checklist

**During Pregnancy:**
- [ ] Review OB, hospital, and anesthesiologist network status
- [ ] Know your deductible and out-of-pocket maximum
- [ ] Confirm STD enrollment and maternity leave benefit
- [ ] Understand FMLA eligibility and plan your leave
- [ ] Apply for/review life insurance
- [ ] Enroll in Dependent Care FSA during open enrollment

**Within 30 Days of Birth:**
- [ ] Add baby to health insurance
- [ ] File STD/disability claim (within employer's deadline)
- [ ] File for state paid leave (if applicable)
- [ ] Apply for Social Security number (hospital may assist)
